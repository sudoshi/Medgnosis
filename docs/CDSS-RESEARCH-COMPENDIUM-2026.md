## 1. CDS Foundations, Taxonomy & Interoperable-CDS Standards

### Overview

Clinical decision support (CDS) has matured from bespoke, institution-locked logic into a discipline organized around two complementary bodies of knowledge: a theory of effectiveness and a stack of computable, shareable standards. The effectiveness theory is anchored by Jerome Osheroff's "Five Rights of CDS" (right information, right person, right format, right channel, right time — articulated in the CDS implementer's guides ~2005-2007 and CMS-referenced as a best-practice QI approach), David Bates' "Ten Commandments for Effective CDS" (JAMIA 2003;10(6):523-530: speed is everything; anticipate and deliver in real time; fit the workflow; favor redirection over hard stops; monitor impact and respond to feedback; maintain the knowledge base), and the GUIDES checklist (Van de Velde et al., Implementation Science 2018;13:86) which adds four governance domains — context, content, system, implementation. These frameworks are the "why" and "how-well"; they make clear that a world-class CDS platform is judged less by raw rule count than by workflow fit, alert-fatigue avoidance, and a closed loop of measurement and maintenance. The corpus of alert-fatigue evidence (override rates of 49-96% in published studies; tiered/interruptive-only-when-actionable designs; AMA/Joint Commission attention to alert burden) is the empirical spine under these frameworks.

The CDS intervention taxonomy spans interruptive alerts/reminders (modal pop-ups, hard/soft stops), non-interruptive nudges (passive InBasket items, relevant-data displays, dashboards), order sets, documentation templates/smart forms, infobuttons and reference info, protocol/pathway support, and increasingly predictive/ML models. The decisive 2003-2025 lesson is that interruptive alerts are overused and drive fatigue; the frontier favors non-interruptive, well-targeted, evidence-graded interventions delivered through the Five Rights lens, with every fired intervention instrumented for feedback (the CDS Hooks /feedback endpoint operationalizes Bates' "monitor and respond"). The CDS Five Rights taxonomy and AHRQ/ONC "CDS intervention types" provide a shared vocabulary every Medgnosis intervention should be classifiable against.

Knowledge representation has a long lineage — HL7 Arden Syntax (rule-based Medical Logic Modules, crippled by the "curly-braces problem" of site-specific data bindings), and the computer-interpretable-guideline (CIG) / Task-Network-Model family GLIF/GLIF3, PROforma, Asbru (intention/time-oriented), EON, GUIDE, PRODIGY, GASTON, plus document-markup GEM and ontology-driven SAGE. The Peleg et al. pattern analysis (J Biomed Inform / PMC2213484) showed none achieved cross-vendor portability (PROforma covered the most control-flow patterns, Asbru next, then GLIF, then EON). The modern convergence is decisive and worth betting on: HL7 Clinical Quality Language (CQL), compiled to ELM (Expression Logical Model) and built as a strict superset of FHIRPath, is now the single authoring language for BOTH quality measurement and CDS. CQL is published at v1.5.3 (STU; R1) with Release 2 (v2.0.0-ballot, build cql.hl7.org/2025Sep) in HL7 Trial-Use ballot (additive, no breaking changes; trial-use content from 1.5 stays trial-use). CQL logic is packaged in FHIR Library resources and orchestrated by the FHIR Clinical Reasoning module — PlanDefinition (workflow/pathway logic), ActivityDefinition (single proposable actions), Measure + MeasureReport (quality), GuidanceResponse (CDS output) — exposed via operations $apply (instantiate a guideline into a CarePlan/RequestGroup for a patient), $evaluate-measure, $care-gaps, and Library/$evaluate. CPG-on-FHIR (Clinical Practice Guidelines IG, hl7.fhir.uv.cpg#2.0.0, FHIR R4) packages all this as evidence-graded "Knowledge Artifacts" via CPGComputablePlanDefinition / CPGRecommendation profiles carrying GRADE-style quality/strength/direction of recommendation.

The interoperability layer that makes CDS executable in real workflows is now production-grade: HL7 CDS Hooks v2.0.1 (current published; an errata republish of 2.0.0 on the FHIR IG Publisher — substantively identical) defines workflow-triggered services with hooks now published as independent specs under a Hook Maturity Model (patient-view, order-select, order-sign, order-dispatch, appointment-book, encounter-start, encounter-discharge, medication-prescribe and others), a discovery endpoint ({baseUrl}/cds-services), prefetch, Cards (summary/detail/indicator/source/suggestions/links), systemActions (auto-applied), overrideReasons, and a /feedback endpoint; SMART App Launch v2.2.0 (STU 2.2) supplies the OAuth2 app-launch, scope, and launch-context substrate; and HL7 Infobutton (Context-Aware Knowledge Retrieval, URL-Based IG) covers reference info. The sharing infrastructure has shifted: AHRQ's CDS Connect public site (cds.ahrq.gov) and the federal repository went offline ~April 28, 2025 amid federal realignment, but the work continues as HL7-community-owned "CDS Connect Community Edition," including the new CQL Studio web suite for authoring/testing/publishing CQL+FHIR artifacts; VSAC remains the value-set source of truth; and the cqframework reference engines (clinical-reasoning, cqf-ruler) complete the "author → share → implement" lifecycle. Regulatory gravity is pulling everyone here: CMS is transitioning eCQMs (QDM v5.6-based) to FHIR-based digital quality measures (dQMs / FHIR eCQMs in CQL+QI-Core), and CMS-0057-F mandates four FHIR APIs (Patient Access, Provider Access, Payer-to-Payer, Prior Authorization) and the Da Vinci CRD/DTR/PAS prior-auth IGs — with the API/prior-auth provisions due in production by January 1, 2027 and certain provisions effective January 1, 2026. CRD is literally a CDS Hooks service.

For Medgnosis specifically, this stream defines the single highest-leverage architectural pivot available — and the codebase confirms the team has already laid the seam for it. Medgnosis has the hardest, most differentiating pieces — a 1M-patient hybrid EDW/star warehouse (phm_edw 3NF + phm_star Kimball), a large eCQM/measure catalog (CMS2-CMS951; README cites 48 eCQM definitions, broader catalog ~753 rows incl. strata in migrations 051-054), a multi-bundle care-gap engine with real-time WebSocket updates, a published CDS Hooks 2.0 discovery endpoint already serving two services (medgnosis-care-gaps on order-sign; medgnosis-problem-list on patient-view), live VSAC value-set tables (migration 050), and read-only FHIR R4. Crucially, apps/api/src/services/measureEvaluator.ts already defines a MeasureEvaluator seam ('sql' today, 'cql' stub) explicitly designed to swap in a cqf-ruler/clinical-reasoning bridge with no caller change. But its measures are still hand-authored SQL and its rules are an EAV "logic-as-data" engine (phm_edw.clinical_rule) — both of which are, structurally, exactly the Arden-era trap of non-portable bespoke logic (the EAV engine is even self-described in code as "the Geisinger doctrine"). The decisive move is to make logic computable-and-shareable by adopting CQL+ELM (executed via an embedded cqframework/clinical-reasoning JVM sidecar rather than a hand-built engine), profiling FHIR to US Core 6.1/7.0 and QI-Core, emitting FHIR Measure/MeasureReport via $evaluate-measure, and re-expressing the care-gap engine as Da Vinci DEQM (STU5/v5.0.0) $care-gaps. This converts Medgnosis from "an excellent proprietary analytics app" into "a standards-conformant knowledge platform that can author, share, ingest, and execute the same artifacts the rest of the ecosystem uses" — and incidentally unlocks CMS dQM submission and the CMS-0057-F prior-auth market via CRD/DTR atop the CDS Hooks substrate it already has.

### Key Findings

- **The Five Rights of CDS (Osheroff) is the canonical effectiveness lens and is CMS/AHRQ-referenced** — Successful CDS delivers the right information, to the right person, in the right format (alert/order set/infobutton/dashboard), through the right channel (EHR/mobile/portal), at the right time in the workflow. Articulated by Jerome Osheroff MD and colleagues in the HIMSS 'Improving Outcomes with CDS' implementer guides (~2005-2007); AHRQ/CMS reference it as a best-practice health-IT-enabled QI framework. It is an evaluation rubric, not a technology — every Medgnosis intervention should be classifiable against all five rights. CORRECTION vs draft: the '2007' single-date and 'CMS-endorsed' framing is soft; it is more accurately a HIMSS/Osheroff guide popularized 2005-2009 and adopted into AHRQ/ONC CDS guidance.
  - _Evidence:_ AHRQ Digital Healthcare Research, 'CDS Five Rights' (digital.ahrq.gov); Osheroff et al., 'Improving Outcomes with Clinical Decision Support: An Implementer's Guide' (HIMSS, 2nd ed. 2012)
- **Bates' Ten Commandments and the GUIDES checklist define implementation and governance discipline** — Bates et al. (JAMIA 2003;10(6):523-530): speed is everything; anticipate needs and deliver in real time; fit the workflow; little things matter (usability); recognize that physicians resist stopping — favor redirection over hard stops; simple interventions work best; ask for additional info sparingly; monitor impact, get feedback, and respond; manage and maintain the knowledge base. GUIDES (Van de Velde et al., Implementation Science 2018;13:86, doi 10.1186/s13012-018-0772-3) adds 4 governance domains (context, content, system, implementation), each with ~4 factors. Together they justify a non-interruptive, feedback-instrumented, actively-maintained CDS posture.
  - _Evidence:_ JAMIA 2003;10(6):523-530 (Bates), PMC264429; Implementation Science 2018;13:86, PMC6019508 (GUIDES)
- **Alert fatigue is the dominant failure mode — override rates of 49-96% are documented, motivating tiered/non-interruptive design** — Across published EHR studies, clinicians override the large majority of interruptive alerts (commonly cited 49-96% override rate; drug-allergy and drug-drug interaction alerts among the worst). This is the empirical basis for the frontier's move to tiered alerts (interrupt only on high-severity/actionable), non-interruptive surfacing, and mandatory override-reason capture + feedback loops. ADDED finding (absent from draft): any Medgnosis interruptive intervention must capture overrideReasons (CDS Hooks supports this) and feed them back via /feedback so alert burden is itself a measured, governed quantity.
  - _Evidence:_ van der Sijs et al., 'Overriding of drug safety alerts in CPOE' JAMIA 2006;13(2):138-147; AHRQ PSNet 'Alert Fatigue' primer (psnet.ahrq.gov)
- **CDS Hooks v2.0.1 (current published) is the production standard for workflow-triggered embedded CDS; hooks are now versioned independently under a Hook Maturity Model** — VERIFIED/CORRECTED vs draft: current published is v2.0.1 — an errata republish of 2.0.0 with NO substantive changes, moved onto the standard HL7 FHIR IG Publisher. Hook definitions are NOT part of the core spec; they are independent specifications following the Hook Maturity Model and published separately by HL7 (patient-view, order-select, order-sign, order-dispatch, appointment-book, encounter-start, encounter-discharge, medication-prescribe, etc.). Discovery at {baseUrl}/cds-services. Services define id/hook/title/description/prefetch (FHIR query templates with {{context.patientId}} tokens). Request: hookInstance (UUID), hook, fhirServer, fhirAuthorization (OAuth bearer+scope), context, prefetch. Response = Cards (summary <140 chars; detail GFM markdown; indicator info/warning/critical; source; suggestions with selectionBehavior at-most-one/any; actions create/update/delete; overrideReasons; links absolute/smart) PLUS systemActions (auto-applied) PLUS a /feedback endpoint (accepted/overridden + overrideReason + outcomeTimestamp). Security: HTTPS, asymmetric-signed JWT (ES384/RS384); symmetric/none prohibited. Medgnosis already implements this discovery endpoint with two live services.
  - _Evidence:_ cds-hooks.hl7.org (v2.0.1 home + changes.html); cds-hooks.hl7.org/2.0/; cds-hooks.hl7.org/ghp/ (hook catalog / maturity model)
- **CQL is published at v1.5.3 (R1 STU); Release 2 is in trial-use ballot — it is the converged computable-knowledge language for BOTH CDS and quality measurement** — CORRECTION vs draft: the current OFFICIALLY PUBLISHED CQL is v1.5.3 (not a vague 'v1.5.x'); v2.0.0-ballot (R2 STU1, build cql.hl7.org/2025Sep) is a Trial-Use ballot with substantive new features but no breaking changes (trial-use content from 1.5 remains trial-use). CQL is a domain-specific authoring language for measure and CDS artifact authors; it compiles to ELM (Expression Logical Model, canonical XML/JSON) for machine execution and sharing, and is a strict superset of FHIRPath. Grammar is ANTLR4-defined. This is the explicit replacement for bespoke SQL: ONE Library can drive both an eCQM and a CDS rule.
  - _Evidence:_ cql.hl7.org (published v1.5.3); cql.hl7.org/2025Sep (v2.0.0-ballot); build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/cql; github.com/cqframework/clinical_quality_language

Resources: PlanDefinition (pathway/decision logic), ActivityDefinition (single action), Library (CQL/ELM), Measure + MeasureReport, GuidanceResponse. Operations: PlanDefinition/$apply (instantiate guideline → CarePlan/RequestGroup for a patient), ActivityDefinition/$apply, Measure/$evaluate-measure, Library/$evaluate. The CQF initiative targets normative status for Library/Measure/ActivityDefinition/PlanDefinition in a future FHIR release (R6 track). CPG-on-FHIR profiles these as 'Knowledge Artifacts' with CPGComputablePlanDefinition / CPGRecommendation carrying qualityOfEvidence / strengthOfRecommendation / directionOfRecommendation extensions (GRADE-style), and a cpg-enabled extension distinguishing registered vs actively-applied artifacts.

Medgnosis's EAV phm_edw.clinical_rule engine, while elegant for versioned 'logic-as-data' and time-travel, is structurally the same non-portable trap: its logic cannot be exported, shared, or executed by any other system. Keep it as the runtime cache/threshold store, but author the canonical logic in CQL.

### Standards & Protocols

- HL7 CDS Hooks v2.0.1 — current published spec (errata republish of 2.0.0 on the FHIR IG Publisher; no substantive change) for workflow-triggered CDS services, Cards, systemActions, and /feedback.
- CDS Hooks Hook Maturity Model — hooks (patient-view, order-select, order-sign, order-dispatch, appointment-book, encounter-start, encounter-discharge, medication-prescribe, …) are published independently of the core spec, each at its own maturity level.
- HL7 Clinical Quality Language (CQL) v1.5.3 — current PUBLISHED release (R1 STU); v2.0.0-ballot (R2 STU1, cql.hl7.org/2025Sep) in HL7 Trial-Use ballot, additive/non-breaking.
- Expression Logical Model (ELM) — canonical machine-executable XML/JSON compilation target of CQL; what engines actually run and share.
- FHIRPath — path/expression language CQL is a strict superset of; underpins both FHIR and CQL.
- FHIR R4 (4.0.1) Clinical Reasoning module — Library, PlanDefinition, ActivityDefinition, Measure, MeasureReport, GuidanceResponse + $apply, $evaluate-measure, $care-gaps, Library/$evaluate.
- CPG-on-FHIR — Clinical Practice Guidelines IG, hl7.fhir.uv.cpg#2.0.0 (FHIR R4); CPGComputablePlanDefinition / CPGRecommendation with GRADE-style quality/strength/direction extensions.

### Frontier & Trends

- Convergence on a single computable-knowledge stack: CQL → ELM, packaged in FHIR Library/PlanDefinition, executed by FHIR Clinical Reasoning, graded by CPG-on-FHIR. One artifact now serves measurement, CDS, case reporting, and documentation templates ('author once, use many').
- Quality measurement is going fully FHIR/digital: CMS dQMs (FHIR eCQMs in CQL+QI-Core) are replacing QDM-based eCQMs and QRDA; FHIR MeasureReport submission is the mid-term mandate.
- Regulation is the forcing function: CMS-0057-F makes FHIR APIs + Da Vinci prior-auth IGs (CRD = CDS Hooks) production requirements by Jan 1, 2027 — pulling the entire ecosystem onto the same substrate.
- Alert-fatigue science is reshaping CDS UX: tiered/interruptive-only-when-actionable, non-interruptive surfacing, mandatory override-reason capture, and closed-loop /feedback measurement of alert burden itself.
- Federal hosting is retrenching but open source persists: AHRQ CDS Connect's public site went dark (Apr 2025); the community carries it forward as CDS Connect Community Edition + CQL Studio — signaling that durable CDS infrastructure must be self-hostable open source, not dependent on a government portal.
- Prospective ('gaps through period') care-gap detection via DEQM $care-gaps is becoming the payer-exchangeable norm, displacing proprietary gap engines.
- Predictive/ML CDS is increasingly delivered THROUGH the same standards rails (CDS Hooks Cards, GuidanceResponse) rather than as siloed dashboards — and is drawing regulatory scrutiny (transparency, monitoring) that maps onto Bates' 'monitor and respond' and GUIDES governance.
- Normative maturation: the CQF initiative is driving Library/Measure/ActivityDefinition/PlanDefinition toward normative status in a future FHIR release, reducing the 'trial-use churn' risk of betting on these resources.

### Risks & Pitfalls

- Building a TypeScript CQL/ELM engine from scratch is a multi-year trap — the JVM cqframework/clinical-reasoning + cqf-ruler engines (or, for lighter Node interop, fqm-execution) exist and are battle-tested. Adopt, don't reinvent.
- The EAV 'logic-as-data' rules engine (phm_edw.clinical_rule), though elegant for versioning/time-travel, is structurally the Arden-era non-portability trap: its logic cannot be exported, shared, or executed elsewhere. Treat it as a runtime cache, not the canonical authoring format.
- CQL is worthless without the data substrate: logic is authored against QI-Core/US Core. If Medgnosis's FHIR layer isn't profiled to US Core and mapped to QI-Core, CQL libraries won't bind to the EDW. The data-model work is the real cost, not the engine.
- Version churn: CQL R2 (v2.0.0) and CPG/DEQM are still STU/trial-use; pin published versions (CQL 1.5.3, DEQM 5.0.0, CPG 2.0.0, CDS Hooks 2.0.1, SMART 2.2.0) and isolate behind the measureEvaluator-style seam to absorb upgrades.
- CMS submission is gated on conformance Medgnosis lacks today: only QRDA or FHIR MeasureReport are submittable — a raw SQL engine cannot submit, regardless of measure correctness.
- The AHRQ CDS Connect public repo/authoring tool is OFFLINE (since ~Apr 28 2025). Any plan that assumes the AHRQ-hosted site/repo will break; target the open-source Community Edition / CQL Studio and self-host.
- CDS Hooks security hard requirements: HTTPS + asymmetric-signed JWT (ES384/RS384); symmetric/none are prohibited. Medgnosis's current bespoke JWT will not satisfy CDS Hooks fhirAuthorization or SMART App Launch without rework.
- Alert fatigue will silently kill clinical value: shipping more interruptive cards without override-reason capture + /feedback instrumentation reproduces the 49-96% override failure mode the literature warns against.
- Unverified external specifics: the draft's '49 EC + 17 HI + 4 HO dQM draft / comment period through Feb 25 2026' counts could not be independently confirmed here — verify against mmshub.cms.gov before quoting. Likewise the exact Peleg control-flow pattern counts (22/20/17/11) should be re-checked against PMC2213484 before publishing as precise figures.
- Terminology drift: VSAC value sets are versioned and OID-bound; CQL libraries pinned to stale value-set versions will compute wrong populations. Wire a FHIR terminology service ($expand/$validate-code) and version-pin value sets per measure period.

## 2. The Electronic Quality-Measurement Stack — CQL, QDM v5.6, eCQM/dQM Lifecycle

### Overview

The US electronic clinical quality measurement (eCQM) stack is a tightly-coupled, federally-governed pipeline with four conceptual layers: (1) a clinical data model — historically QDM (Quality Data Model), now transitioning to QI-Core FHIR profiles; (2) an expression language — CQL (Clinical Quality Language), HL7's ANSI-normative logic syntax shared by both quality measurement and clinical decision support; (3) a measure-packaging format — historically HQMF (Health Quality Measure Format) XML, now the FHIR Measure + Library resources; and (4) a results/reporting format — historically QRDA Categories I (patient-level) and III (aggregate), now the FHIR MeasureReport via the Da Vinci Data Exchange for Quality Measures (DEQM) IG. Governance runs through the CMS Measures Management System (MMS) Blueprint (currently v17, five-stage lifecycle: conceptualization → specification → testing → implementation → use/continuing-evaluation/maintenance, now delivered as the interactive CMS MMS Hub), the eCQI Resource Center (HealthIT.gov), and value sets distributed exclusively through NLM's Value Set Authority Center (VSAC). The authoring tool chain was consolidated in mid-2024: the legacy Measure Authoring Tool (MAT) and the Bonnie logic-testing tool were both decommissioned on June 28, 2024, replaced by MADiE (Measure Authoring Development Integrated Environment), which authors and tests both QDM-based and FHIR/QI-Core-based eCQMs in one application. Vendor/EHR certification still runs through Cypress (the official ONC Health IT Certification Program tool for criterion 170.315(c) CQMs), which validates QRDA I/III against the annual CMS Implementation Guides using synthetic test decks. CORRECTION vs draft: Cypress is at v7.4 (June 18, 2025) plus patch v7.4.1 (July 31, 2025) supporting the 2026 reporting period with eCQM value sets as of May 8, 2025 — NOT v7.3 with May 2, 2024 value sets as the draft stated.

QDM v5.6 (January 2021, maintained by ESAC, Inc. for CMS/ONC; the spec PDF is in the Medgnosis repo, confirmed: 22 categories) is the data model used by all current QDM-based eCQMs. It is deliberately logic-free — since QDM 5.0 the data model and the logic were split, with CQL supplying all expression logic. QDM defines 22 categories and ~52 datatypes (e.g., 'Encounter, Performed', 'Medication, Order', 'Laboratory Test, Performed', 'Diagnosis'), each datatype carrying datatype-specific attributes (dosage, supply, frequency, route, refills, result, components) plus actor attributes (performer, recorder, requester, prescriber, dispenser, sender/recipient) added in QDM 5.5. Timing is modeled as four period types — relevantPeriod (default start/stop for 14 datatypes), prevalencePeriod (Diagnosis/Allergy/Symptom onset→abatement), participationPeriod, and locationPeriod — plus point-in-time author dateTime and relevant dateTime. Each data element resolves a category to a value set or Direct Reference Code (DRC) from a standard code system (SNOMED CT for conditions/procedures, RxNorm for meds, LOINC for labs/observations, ICD-10-CM/PCS, CPT/HCPCS). Negation in QDM/CQL is expressed via the negationRationale attribute (a value set explaining why an action was not performed, e.g. medical/patient/system reasons) so a 'not done with a valid reason' can satisfy a measure exclusion. The HQMF then carries only the category + value set/DRC bindings and references the CQL library; the CQL ELM (Expression Logical Model, XML/JSON) is the machine-executable artifact actually run by calculation engines. CQL is at v1.5.3 (ANSI Normative Release 1, mixed normative/trial-use), the current published version; an HL7 CQL v2.0 ballot effort is in progress.

The most consequential trend is the CMS digital quality measure (dQM) strategy: CMS intends to convert all quality measures across all programs to FHIR-based dQMs, with health-plan digitization targeted by ~2030 (NCQA HEDIS is on the same 2026–2030 trajectory). The first step is translating existing QDM-eCQMs to FHIR eCQMs (QI-Core replaces QDM for clinical data; FHIR Measure/Library replace HQMF; DEQM/MeasureReport replace QRDA I/III). The FHIR Quality Measure IG (cqf-measures, currently v5.0.0 STU5, built on CQL R1.5.x and QI-Core, which itself is FHIR-R4-based) defines computable Measure resources with proportion/ratio/continuous-variable/cohort scoring and population criteria (initial-population, denominator, denominator-exclusion/exception, numerator, numerator-exclusion, measure-population, measure-observation) expressed as CQL referenced from Library resources, executed via the $evaluate-measure operation. CORRECTION vs draft: QI-Core is now at STU7 v7.0.2 (errata published April 2026) — STU7 v7.0.0/7.0.1 were published June/July 2025, superseding STU6 v6.0.0 (March 2024); the draft cited 7.0.1 as current. DEQM is at v5.0.0 (2025, FHIR 4.0.1), which the draft did not version. CMS publishes dual content yearly via the cqframework GitHub org (e.g., ecqm-content-qicore-2025, ecqm-content-cms-2026) and runs three annual HL7 FHIR Connectathons. The CMS measure inventory (verified at 508 measures spanning 36 distinct programs, not 35) confirms program breadth: MIPS dominates (217), then MACS 40, MQRS 37, HIQR 34, PI 34, PARTC 34, MCCS 30, NHQI 23, with PAC programs (SNFQRP/IRFQR/LTCHQR/HHQR), hospital programs (IQR, OQR/HOQR, PI, IPFQR, PCHQR, ASCQR), Medicaid Adult/Child Core Sets, Part C/D Stars (HEDIS-based), value-based programs (HVBP, HACRP, HRRP, SNFVBP, HHVBP, ESRD QIP), Shared Savings (MSSP/APP), and Innovation Center models (EOM, KCC, ACO REACH, GUIDE). The CMS Universal Foundation overlays this by defining a common aligned measure set spanning MIPS, MSSP APP/APP Plus, Medicaid Core Sets, Marketplace QRS, and MA/Part D Stars.

For Medgnosis specifically: the platform today executes ~753 measures as hand-authored SQL against the phm_edw/phm_star warehouse with numerator/denominator/exclusion + Wilson CIs — operationally strong, but standards-orphaned. The repo already has a measureEvaluator service, a VSAC integration (migrations 050/052, vsacService — value sets loaded live per PR #1), and a phm_edw.care_gap table feeding CDS Hooks. None of that logic is CQL, none is shareable, and Medgnosis cannot today submit to any CMS program because it lacks QRDA I/III export, FHIR Measure/MeasureReport, QI-Core conformance, and any CQL execution engine. This stream is the single highest-leverage standards investment for Medgnosis's 'most advanced CDSS in the world' thesis, because CQL is the shared substrate of both eCQMs and CDS Hooks logic — adopting a CQL engine simultaneously upgrades the measure engine (shareable, certifiable, CMS-aligned), the CDS rules engine (the existing EAV 'logic-as-data' engine can be re-expressed as CQL/PlanDefinition), and the gaps-in-care engine (Da Vinci DEQM $care-gaps). The strategic move is to embed the cqframework clinical-reasoning library — which is the canonical CQL/Measure engine now upstreamed INTO HAPI FHIR and HAPI FHIR JPA Server Starter (cqf-ruler is effectively superseded; its operations migrated into clinical-reasoning + HAPI) — as a JVM sidecar microservice behind the Fastify API, ingest the published CMS QI-Core eCQM content + VSAC value sets (already partially staged), and emit DEQM MeasureReports. CORRECTION vs draft: prefer clinical-reasoning-in-HAPI over standalone cqf-ruler; the IBM/Alvearie quality-measure-and-cohort-service is dormant (last release 2.0.1, January 2022) and should not be a primary dependency.

### Key Findings

- **QDM and CQL were deliberately decoupled at QDM v5.0; QDM is now a logic-free data model and CQL supplies all expression logic** — QDM v5.6 (Jan 2021, ESAC Inc. for CMS/ONC) defines 22 categories (confirmed from the local spec PDF) and ~52 datatypes but contains NO comparison/timing logic — that lives entirely in CQL. The spec is explicitly subtitled 'FOR USE BY MEASURE DEVELOPERS CREATING MEASURES USING CLINICAL QUALITY LANGUAGE (CQL)'. A QDM data element = Category + Datatype(context) + value set/DRC + attributes; CQL then expresses timing, negation, and population membership. QDM v5.6 remains the active version through the AU2025/2026 reporting cycle; CMS continues dual-publishing QDM-eCQMs and FHIR-eCQMs.
  - _Evidence:_ QDM v5.6 PDF (repo root, /home/smudoshi/Github/Medgnosis/QDM-v5.6-508.pdf): 'The QDM currently contains 22 categories', 'published January 2021'; ecqi.healthit.gov/qdm confirms v5.6 Jan 2021.
- **The authoring toolchain was consolidated in 2024: MAT and Bonnie were both decommissioned June 28, 2024, replaced by MADiE** — MADiE (Measure Authoring Development Integrated Environment) authors AND tests eCQMs in one self-contained app, supporting both QDM-based and FHIR/QI-Core-based measures. ### Standards & Protocols

- QDM v5.6 (Jan 2021) — logic-free clinical data model; 22 categories, ~52 datatypes; active for QDM-eCQMs through AU2025/2026
- CQL v1.5.3 — ANSI Normative Release 1 (mixed normative/trial-use) expression language for measures + CDS; compiles to ELM; HL7 CQL v2.0 in balloting
- ELM (Expression Logical Model) — canonical machine-readable XML/JSON form of CQL that engines execute
- HQMF (Health Quality Measure Format) — legacy XML measure package, being replaced by FHIR Measure/Library
- HL7 FHIR Quality Measure IG (cqf-measures) v5.0.0 STU5 — computable Measure + Library; $evaluate-measure; proportion/ratio/CV/cohort scoring
- QI-Core IG STU7 v7.0.2 (errata Apr 2026; STU7 base Jun 2025) — FHIR R4 clinical data model replacing QDM; v8.0.0 in ballot; built on US Core
- US Core IG v7.0.0 (STU7) — base US-Realm profiles QI-Core constrains; the (g)(10) certified-API data baseline
- Da Vinci DEQM IG v5.0.0 (2025, FHIR 4.0.1) — Individual/Summary MeasureReport (QRDA I/III replacement), $care-gaps, collect-data/submit-data
- HL7 CRMI IG — canonical knowledge-artifact versioning/packaging/distribution ($package, $data-requirements) for Measure/Library/ValueSet/PlanDefinition
- QRDA Category I — patient-level results XML (HL7 CDA-based); 2026 CMS QRDA I IG for HQR
- QRDA Category III — aggregate results XML; 2025/2026 CMS QRDA III EC IG for MIPS/APP
- QPP JSON — MIPS/APP submission payload (only format MIPS CQMs / Medicare CQMs may use; eCQMs may use QRDA III or QPP JSON)
- CMS MMS Blueprint v17 — measure-development governance; 5-stage lifecycle via the CMS MMS Hub
- CDS Hooks 2.0 — service-call spec for context-aware CDS; shares CQL/PlanDefinition logic with eCQMs
- HL7 FHIR PlanDefinition/ActivityDefinition + $apply — computable CDS artifact form (clinical-reasoning) parallel to Measure
- VSAC value set OID binding + FHIR Terminology Service $expand — value-set distribution keyed to annual reporting-year as-of dates
- Code systems: SNOMED CT, RxNorm, LOINC, ICD-10-CM/PCS, CPT/HCPCS, UCUM — the terminology substrate of value sets and DRCs
- FHIR Bulk Data Access ($export / 'flat FHIR') — the population-scale data-acquisition path for dQMs (collect-data at scale)
- CMS Universal Foundation — aligned core measure set across MIPS, MSSP APP/APP Plus, Medicaid Core Sets, Marketplace QRS, MA/Part D Stars

### Frontier & Trends

- dQM mandate: CMS converting ALL measures to FHIR dQMs; health-plan digitization ~2030; NCQA HEDIS dMeasures on the same 2026-2030 arc — the strategic window to build standards-native now and lead rather than retrofit.
- cqf-ruler → clinical-reasoning-in-HAPI consolidation: the reference engine is being absorbed into HAPI FHIR core, lowering the cost of adopting a conformant Measure/$care-gaps/CDS engine to 'run HAPI JPA Starter'.
- Hybrid measures + NHSN Link API: Sepsis Hybrid dQM moves off QRDA toward FHIR data exchange via the CDC NHSN Link API — signaling FHIR-Bulk-Data/flat-FHIR collect-data as the future submission substrate.
- Universal Foundation convergence: APP Plus growing 6→8→9→11 (2025→2028), Medicaid Adult Core Set and MA/Part D Stars aligning — a smaller, shared, high-value measure set is the realistic first target rather than all 508.
- Hospital Harm measure expansion: CY2026 mandates glycemic harm (CMS0816v5/CMS0871v5); CY2027 proposed rule adds more Hospital Harm + eCQMs — harm measures are a growth area where real-time CDS (Medgnosis's strength) directly improves the measured outcome.
- CQL as the unifying substrate: the same CQL/ELM drives eCQMs, CDS Hooks, and Da Vinci gaps — one engine investment compounds across three product surfaces.
- CRMI-based measure repositories: standards-native versioning/packaging of measures-as-artifacts is maturing, enabling shareable, certifiable measure libraries instead of bespoke SQL.
- Medicare CQM collection type: a transitional, claims-aligned/sampling reporting path for ACOs leaving Web Interface — relevant if Medgnosis serves ACO clients before full eCQM readiness.
- OMOP↔FHIR/CQL bridging: growing interest in executing CQL over OMOP-sourced data (concept_id↔value-set mapping) — directly relevant to Medgnosis/Parthenon's OMOP foundation.
- AI-assisted measure authoring: emerging tooling to translate narrative measure intent → CQL; a natural Medgnosis differentiator given its CDS/AI stack.

### Risks & Pitfalls

- Value-set version drift: using the wrong reporting-year VSAC as-of date silently changes numerators/denominators; Cypress will flag it but only after the fact — pin and store full expansions per reporting year.
- Collection-type confusion in MIPS/APP: eCQM vs MIPS CQM vs Medicare CQM have different formats (QRDA III/QPP JSON vs QPP-JSON-only) and cannot mix within a single measure — encoding the wrong type fails submission.
- Assuming cqf-ruler is current: building on cqf-ruler instead of clinical-reasoning-in-HAPI adopts a superseded server; track HAPI FHIR's clinical-reasoning version instead.
- QDM↔QI-Core mapping is non-trivial: negationRationale, timing periods (relevant/prevalence/participation/location), and attribute semantics do NOT map 1:1 to FHIR — naive translation produces wrong results; use HL7's official QDM v5.6→QI-Core R7 mapping and validate against published dual content.
- Re-authoring instead of ingesting: hand-porting 753 SQL measures to CQL is wasteful where CMS already publishes CQL/ELM — ingest ecqm-content-* bundles for covered measures; reserve net-new CQL for Medgnosis-original logic.
- OMOP/concept_id vs FHIR/value-set-OID mismatch: Medgnosis data is OMOP-vocabulary-keyed; CQL expects code-system codes resolved against VSAC value sets — a terminology bridge (concept_id → source code → value-set membership) is mandatory and easy to get subtly wrong.
- JVM sidecar operational cost: clinical-reasoning is Java; adds a non-Node runtime, memory footprint, and ops surface to a Fastify/Node + PHP shop — budget for it or accept the less-complete JS engine path.
- ELM staleness: shipping ELM compiled with an old translator can diverge from current CQL semantics — recompile ELM in CI against a pinned translator version per release.
- Certification ≠ submission: passing Cypress certifies calculation correctness but does not enroll Medgnosis in any CMS program; program participation requires the MMS Blueprint endorsement/rulemaking path for original measures.
- Underestimating QRDA I CDA complexity: QRDA Category I is CDA/XML with strict template constraints (not FHIR); generating valid QRDA I from FHIR/MeasureReport requires a dedicated transform and is a common failure point.
- Treating Universal Foundation as static: the aligned set and APP Plus expand yearly (2025→2028) — a hard-coded measure list will silently fall behind annual rulemaking.
- Negation/exclusion logic errors: 'not done with valid reason' (negationRationale) is the single most error-prone CQL pattern; mis-modeling it inflates or deflates exclusions and is a frequent Cypress failure.

### Implications for Medgnosis

- Stand up a clinical-reasoning-in-HAPI JVM sidecar (HAPI FHIR JPA Server Starter + cqframework clinical-reasoning, Docker) behind the Fastify API, exposing $evaluate-measure, $care-gaps, and CDS Hooks from one engine — this is the foundational build for this stream. Do NOT build on the superseded cqf-ruler or the dormant IBM/Alvearie engine.
- Build an OMOP↔FHIR/QI-Core projection layer: map phm_edw/phm_star to QI-Core STU7 resources and concept_id→source-code→VSAC-value-set membership, so the existing warehouse becomes the data source the CQL engine evaluates against. This terminology bridge is the long pole.
- Ingest CMS-published FHIR eCQM content directly: pull ecqm-content-qicore-2025/ecqm-content-cms-2026 (CQL, ELM, Measure/Library, value sets) and the matching VSAC expansions rather than re-authoring covered measures — reserve net-new CQL only for Medgnosis-original logic.
- Emit DEQM artifacts: convert the existing phm_edw.care_gap output into DEQM v5.0.0 $care-gaps Gaps-In-Care Bundles (with gapStatus open/closed) and DEQM Individual/Summary MeasureReports — making Medgnosis's gaps engine standards-native and exchange-ready.
- Add a QRDA/QPP export module: generate QRDA Category III XML + QPP JSON (EC/MIPS/APP) and QRDA Category I (hospital), then validate against Cypress v7.4.x CVU+ in CI as the objective conformance oracle before claiming 'submission-ready'.
- Model the measure library as CRMI knowledge artifacts: version Medgnosis measures as FHIR Measure/Library with draft/active/retired states and semantic versions, pinned to reporting-year VSAC as-of dates — turning 753 bespoke SQL measures into a shareable, certifiable repository over time.
- Compile CQL→ELM in CI with a pinned cqframework translator version, checking ELM into the measure repo so runtime engine choice (JVM clinical-reasoning vs JS cqf-execution) stays decoupled from authoring and ELM never drifts from current CQL semantics.

## 3. HL7 FHIR QI-Core — The Quality Data Model on FHIR

### Overview

QI-Core (Quality Improvement Core) is the HL7 FHIR US-Realm implementation guide that defines the standardized data model for clinical quality measurement and clinical decision support. Its purpose is twofold and explicit in the IG: support electronic Clinical Quality Measures / digital quality measures (eCQMs/dQMs) for retrospective measurement, and support Clinical Decision Support (CDS) authored in CQL (Clinical Quality Language) for prospective guidance. QI-Core is built ON TOP OF US Core: each clinical profile constrains the corresponding US Core profile and adds quality-relevant elements, so "conforming to US Core automatically satisfies a significant subset of the conformance requirements of QI-Core." VERIFIED (hl7.org/fhir/us/qicore/, fetched 2026-06-13): the current published edition is QI-Core 7.0.2 (STU 7, "Generated 2026-04-04") on FHIR R4 (4.0.1), aligning with US Core STU 7. CORRECTION: US Core 7.0.0 anchors USCDI v4 (ONC-published July 2023), not USCDI v3 — the draft's loose "USCDI-anchored" understated the version. The next baseline is QI-Core 8.0.0-ballot in the CI build (build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-qi-core/), which aligns with US Core 8.0.0 / USCDI v5. QI-Core historically harmonizes the legacy Quality Data Model (QDM) and the virtual Medical Record (vMR), and CMS is actively transitioning QDM-based eCQMs to QI-Core/FHIR-based dQMs (CMS dQM roadmap; ecqi.healthit.gov/dqm).\n\nThe profile set spans ~50 profiles covering the full clinical record: QICore Patient, Encounter, two Condition profiles (Encounter Diagnosis and Problems-Health Concerns), Coverage, Claim/ClaimResponse, AllergyIntolerance, Medication, MedicationRequest, MedicationAdministration, MedicationDispense, MedicationStatement, Procedure, Immunization (+ImmunizationEvaluation/Recommendation), ServiceRequest, DeviceRequest, Device, multiple Observation profiles (Laboratory Result, Clinical Result, Screening Assessment, Simple, NonPatient), DiagnosticReport (Lab/Note), CarePlan, CareTeam, Goal, Flag, Task, Communication/CommunicationRequest, ImagingStudy, AdverseEvent, FamilyMemberHistory, BodyStructure, Specimen, Substance, NutritionOrder, Practitioner/PractitionerRole, Organization, Location, RelatedPerson, QuestionnaireResponse. The single most important construct for measurement is the negation pattern, because measure denominator exclusions/exceptions depend on representing intentional \"not done.\" VERIFIED against hl7.org/fhir/us/qicore/negation.html: completed-activity negation uses status=not-done plus statusReason, via named profiles QICore Communication Not Done, QICore Immunization Not Done, QICore MedicationAdministration Not Done, QICore MedicationDispense Declined, and QICore Procedure Not Done; order-not-to-perform uses doNotPerform=true plus reasonCode through the three Prohibited profiles QICore Medication Prohibited (MedicationRequest), QICore Service Prohibited (ServiceRequest), and QICore Device Prohibited (DeviceRequest). Rejected proposals use a two-resource model (a request with intent=proposal plus a QICore Task Rejected with status=rejected, focus referencing the proposal, plus statusReason). Negation reasons bind to ValueSet qicore-negation-reason (SNOMED: 183966005 Drug treatment not indicated; 416406003 Procedure discontinued; 275936005 Patient noncompliance - general), and a codeOptions extension lets one instance assert that none of a value-set's members occurred.\n\nCQL authors do not write against raw FHIR; they write against QUICK, a profile-informed logical view over QI-Core that exposes author-friendly type names, treats extensions/slices as first-class elements, and lets primitives be used without a .value accessor. The machine binding is QICore-ModelInfo, a CQL model-definition library that maps the author-friendly names to QI-Core StructureDefinitions and declares primary code and date paths. CORRECTION on versioning: the QI-Core 7.0.2 IG ships its own version-matched ModelInfo declaring `using QICore version '7.0.2'` (verified at modelinfo.html). The separately published us-cql-ig (\"Common CQL Artifacts for FHIR (US-Based)\", now v2.0.0) carries a QICore-ModelInfo snapshot still labeled \"QI Core version 6.0.0\" — so the draft's flat \"6.0.0-derived\" claim conflated two artifacts; implementers MUST pin ModelInfo to the QI-Core version their CMS measures target (7.0.x). A CQL engine loads QICore-ModelInfo (plus FHIRHelpers as a REQUIRED RUNTIME dependency, FHIRCommon and QICoreCommon for shared functions like toInterval/abatementInterval/prevalenceInterval) to translate a retrieve such as [Procedure: \"Flexible Sigmoidoscopy\"] into a FHIR query that resolves against QI-Core data. CQL itself is at specification v1.5.3 (R1 Errata 2, cql.hl7.org). A complete FHIR eCQM combines QI-Core (DATA model) with the cqf-measures IG v5.0.0 (MEASURE packaging: a FHIR Measure resource plus a FHIR Library carrying CQL/ELM, with the cqfm-populationBasis extension declaring patient-based vs non-patient-based counting). Results are produced by the $evaluate-measure operation as a MeasureReport, and Da Vinci DEQM v5.0.0 (STU5) governs MeasureReport exchange (individual / subject-list / summary) and Gaps in Care. The reference execution stack is cqframework's JavaScript engines (fqm-execution, built on cql-execution), which CMS's MADiE authoring tool uses (MADiE updated to fqm-execution 1.8.3); a critical operational fact is that current engines REQUIRE all value sets to be pre-expanded during measure execution. To claim \"QI-Core support for measure data,\" a system must: produce/consume resources that validate against the QI-Core (and transitively US Core) StructureDefinitions; populate Must Support elements per Must-Support sender/receiver rules; honor QI-Core terminology bindings; correctly implement the negation pattern so exclusions compute; and expose data at the paths QICore-ModelInfo expects so CQL retrieves resolve.\n\nFor Medgnosis specifically, QI-Core is the missing keystone that converts its considerable existing assets into standards-conformant, CMS-submittable capability. VERIFIED in the local repo (/home/smudoshi/Github/Medgnosis): there is an unused QDM-v5.6-508.pdf at repo root, a CMS Measure Inventory Export xlsx, VSAC value sets staged via migrations 050_vsac_value_sets.sql through 053 (load-vsac.sh present), measure infrastructure in migrations 051_measure_strata/052_measure_value_set_roles/054_dedup_measure_definition, and NO qicore/$evaluate-measure/CQL engine code anywhere under apps/. Medgnosis today executes its measure library as hand-authored SQL against phm_edw with numerator/denominator/exclusion and Wilson CIs, but has NO CQL engine, NO QI-Core profiling (FHIR resources are ad hoc and read-only), and NO FHIR Measure/MeasureReport or $evaluate-measure. The strategically correct path is to NOT re-author SQL measures into CQL and NOT build QDM logic; instead stand up a QI-Core FHIR facade over phm_edw, load the already-staged VSAC value sets behind a terminology service that pre-expands ($expand/$validate-code), embed a CQL engine (fqm-execution / cql-execution) that loads QICore-ModelInfo pinned to 7.0.2, and then ingest and execute CMS-published QI-Core FHIR eCQMs (authored in MADiE) unchanged. Because QI-Core serves BOTH measurement and CDS, the same CQL can drive Medgnosis's nightly BullMQ measure batch (retrospective $evaluate-measure → MeasureReport) AND its existing CDS Hooks care-gap cards (prospective), unifying its measure engine, care-gap engine, and clinical_rule CDS engine on one data model and one logic language. Emitting cqf-measures Measure/Library + DEQM MeasureReport (and mapping the existing care-gap engine to Da Vinci Gaps in Care) closes the dQM-submission gap, while the existing SQL engine can be retained as a fast-path cache reconciled against the conformant CQL results to validate the facade.

### Frontier & Trends

- CMS dQM transition: QDM eCQMs are being converted to QI-Core/FHIR eCQMs (May 2025 draft for 2026 reporting year); a 2026 public-comment window (through Feb 23, 2026) and dual QDM/FHIR submission glide path mean QI-Core is the forward target — building on QDM now is dead-ended.
- Version step coming: QI-Core 8.0.0 / US Core 8.0.0 / USCDI v5 in ballot; implementers must plan ModelInfo and profile re-pinning as CMS measures move to the 8.x baseline.
- R4 is sticky for regulation: QI-Core deliberately stays on FHIR R4 (4.0.1) for ASTP/ONC alignment; R4B/R5 work exists upstream but is not a near-term measurement requirement.
- Engine convergence on mandatory value-set expansion: fqm-execution/cql-execution now require pre-expanded value sets, pushing terminology-service pre-expansion (or cached expansions) into the critical path of any execution architecture.
- Unification of measurement and CDS on one substrate: QI-Core + CQL + (Measure / PlanDefinition $apply) lets retrospective measures and prospective CDS Hooks cards share data model and logic — the dominant architectural pattern for modern quality+CDS platforms.
- MADiE-centered content supply chain: CMS measure stewards publish executable FHIR Measure/Library bundles + test decks (cqframework dqm-content-qicore-20xx repos), shifting implementers from authoring measures to ingesting-and-executing them.

### Risks & Pitfalls

- ModelInfo version mismatch: loading the us-cql-ig 6.0.0 QICore-ModelInfo against QI-Core 7.0.2 measures causes silent retrieve mis-resolution or CQL-to-ELM translation failures. Pin ModelInfo to the exact QI-Core version of the target measures.
- Forgetting value-set pre-expansion: fqm-execution requires ALL value sets expanded at execution time; an un-expanded VSAC set yields empty retrieves and silently wrong (often zero) numerators/denominators.
- Negation done wrong = broken exclusions: if not-done/statusReason and doNotPerform/reasonCode are not represented exactly per the named Prohibited/Not-Done profiles, denominator exclusions/exceptions silently fail to compute, inflating or deflating measure scores.
- Treating QI-Core as US Core: skipping the QI-Core quality delta (Key Element flags, negation profiles, quality-specific Must Support) means resources validate as US Core but fail QI-Core conformance and fail to drive measures.
- Population basis omission: not declaring cqfm-populationBasis (patient-based vs non-patient-based) makes MeasureReport counts ambiguous and non-conformant; encounter-based measures mis-count if treated as patient-based.
- Two-engine drift: running both a Java (clinical-reasoning) and JS (fqm-execution) engine, or a SQL fast-path and a CQL path, risks divergent results unless one is authoritative and the other reconciled against it.
- R5/R4B premature adoption: chasing R5 breaks alignment with CMS measures that are authored against R4 QI-Core 7.x.
- Must Support receiver rule violations: receivers that error on absent Must Support elements (rather than processing gracefully) break conformance.
- Building QDM logic: investing in the repo's QDM v5.6 PDF duplicates effort CMS is retiring; the conversion target is QI-Core.
- Terminology drift: VSAC value sets are versioned/dated per measurement period; executing a current measure against stale expansions produces incorrect cohorts.
- Read-only/ad-hoc FHIR facade gaps: if the facade does not expose data at exactly the paths QICore-ModelInfo declares (primary code/date paths), CQL retrieves resolve empty even when phm_edw has the data.

## 4. QRDA Category I & III — Versions, Structure & CMS Submission

### Overview

QRDA (Quality Reporting Document Architecture) is the HL7 CDA Release 2-based standard document format for exchanging electronic clinical quality measure (eCQM) data, and it remains the mandatory wire format for nearly all CMS eCQM-based programs through at least the 2026–2027 reporting years. There are two production categories. Category I is a single-patient report: one CDA XML document per patient containing the raw, structured patient-level data elements (encounters, diagnoses, labs, medications, procedures) required to evaluate that patient against one or more measures, plus measure-specific population assignments. Category III is the aggregate/summary report: a single document carrying calculated population counts (numerator, denominator, exclusions, exceptions, performance/reporting rates, stratified counts) per measure for a population over a reporting period. Both are constrained CDA R2 documents with a structured header (record target, author, custodian, legal authenticator, document/section/entry template IDs) and a body of measure sections containing measure-data entries (Cat I) or aggregateCount observations keyed to a measureReference/measure organizer plus reporting-parameters act (Cat III). Both mandate supplemental data elements — sex (Administrative Gender), race (CDC Race & Ethnicity), ethnicity, and payer (Source of Payment Typology / SOP) — stratifying every reported population.

The standards stack has two layers the roadmap must keep distinct. The HL7 base STUs change slowly: QRDA Category I is HL7 CDA R2 IG QRDA I Release 1, STU Release 5.3 (Oct 2021, errata Dec 2022, aligned to QDM v5.6); QRDA Category III is HL7 CDA R2 IG QRDA III Release 1 (Sept 2021). There is NO published STU 5.4 — 5.3 + errata is the live base for every CMS program year 2024–2027. On top of these, CMS publishes a new Implementation Guide, Schematron, and sample-file package every program year that layers CMS-specific conformance rules (the CMS_#### validation rules) and the year's measure set. Corrected, verified version matrix (eCQI /qrda/versions, fetched June 2026): Cat I — 2024 v1.1 (Aug 2023, Schematron Aug 2023), 2025 v1.1 (republished May 2025; Schematron May 2024), 2026 v1.0 (May 2025; Schematron May 2025), 2027 v1.0 PUBLISHED/FINAL (May 2026; Schematron May 2026; adds the new Rural Emergency Hospital Quality Reporting Program, REHQR). Cat III — 2024 v1.1 (Nov 2023; Schematron Feb 2024), 2025 v1.1 (Dec 2024; Schematron Dec 2024), 2026 v1.1 (Dec 2025; Schematron July 2025); the DRAFT 2027 Cat III IG was out for public comment May 18–June 8, 2026. NOTE the draft brief conflated the two 2027 comment windows: the 2027 Cat I draft comment period closed March 25, 2026 and the final 2027 Cat I IG already shipped (May 2026); only the 2027 Cat III IG is still in draft. The CMS layer is where all annual churn lives.

Submission splits by setting. Hospitals submit QRDA Category I (patient-level) for Hospital Inpatient Quality Reporting (IQR), Outpatient Quality Reporting (OQR), Medicare Promoting Interoperability, and (from CY2027) REHQR through the Hospital Quality Reporting (HQR) system at hqr.cms.gov (on QualityNet), which validates against the CMS IG + Schematron and the HL7 STU 5.3 conformance statements, requiring the sdtc-namespace-enhanced CDA_SDTC.xsd schema. Hospitals additionally submit Hybrid measure data — the Hybrid Hospital-Wide Readmission (HWR, CMS529v6) and Hybrid Hospital-Wide Mortality (HWM) measures — as QRDA Cat I carrying Core Clinical Data Elements (6 vital signs + 7 lab results) plus claims-linking variables; Hybrid HWR/HWM are MANDATORY for IQR (FY2026). Eligible clinicians/groups submit QRDA Category III (aggregate) for MIPS / APM Performance Pathway (APP) / MVPs / Primary Care First through the Quality Payment Program portal and QPP Submissions API; CMS provides an open-source QRDA III Conversion Tool (CMSgov/qpp-conversion-tool, built by Flexion) that transforms QRDA III XML into the QPP JSON scoring format via CLI or REST API — but CRITICALLY its QPP JSON output does NOT carry supplemental data elements or reporting stratifications for MIPS programs (out of scope). The Cypress Validation Utility + Calculation Check (CVU+), shipped inside Cypress (v7.4 supports the 2026 reporting period, value sets as of May 8 2025), at cypressdemo.healthit.gov and on GitHub (projectcypress/cypress), is the official pre-submission validator for both categories and all programs (MIPS/APP/MVP/PCF/HQR/OQR/PI). Authoring/testing has consolidated into MADiE (Measure Authoring Development Integrated Environment), which replaced both the Measure Authoring Tool (MAT) and Bonnie on June 28, 2024 and authors in both QDM and FHIR. Validation distinguishes hard errors (reject the file) from warnings (accepted with flags).

Strategically, CMS is executing a multi-year transition from CDA/QRDA toward FHIR-based digital quality measures (dQMs): QI-Core-profiled FHIR R4 data (QI-Core replaces QDM), CQL logic packaged per the HL7 cqf-measures Quality Measure IG (currently PUBLISHED STU5 / v5.0.0, hl7.fhir.us.cqfmeasures#5.0.0 on FHIR 4.0.1 — not a build), reporting via HL7 Data Exchange for Quality Measures (DEQM) MeasureReport profiles — the DEQM Individual MeasureReport is the explicit FHIR replacement for QRDA Cat I and the Summary MeasureReport for QRDA Cat III — fed by FHIR Bulk Data ($export). CMS opened a draft FHIR dQM public comment period that closed Feb 25, 2026 (now past); QRDA/CDA remains the production mandate today while FHIR/dQM is pilot/voluntary. For Medgnosis this stream is a direct, high-leverage gap-closer. Medgnosis already computes numerator/denominator/exclusion counts, performance rates, and Wilson CIs with strata (verified: phm_star.fact_measure_result + phm_star.fact_measure_strata, migrations 002/051; measureCalculatorV2 aggregates fact_patient_bundle_detail) — exactly the computed payload a QRDA Cat III report carries — yet it has no QRDA Cat I or Cat III export and cannot electronically submit to any CMS program. Its FHIR surface is GET-only (Patient, Condition, Observation, $everything; verified apps/api/src/routes/fhir/index.ts), unprofiled (no QI-Core, no MeasureReport), so Medgnosis sits firmly on the legacy side. The fastest credible path to regulatory relevance is to emit standards-conformant QRDA Cat III from fact_measure_result/fact_measure_strata, validate against the CMS Schematron + CVU+, ship the year-keyed CMS IG version per reporting period, and pipe it through the qpp-conversion-tool — while patching that tool's supplemental-data/stratification gap downstream. In parallel, stand up a FHIR dQM lane (QI-Core profiling, DEQM Individual/Summary MeasureReport, $evaluate-measure, Bulk Data $export) so Medgnosis rides the CDA→FHIR migration rather than rebuilding twice. The QDM v5.6 PDF in the repo root is the spec defining the Cat I data-element semantics it must satisfy.

### Standards & Protocols

- HL7 CDA R2 IG: QRDA Category I, Release 1, STU Release 5.3 (Oct 2021, errata Dec 2022) — single-patient eCQM report; aligned to QDM v5.6; no STU 5.4 exists
- HL7 CDA R2 IG: QRDA Category III, Release 1 (Sept 2021) — aggregate/summary eCQM report (aggregateCount observations + measure organizers + reporting-parameters act)
- CMS QRDA I IG for HQR — annual, year-keyed: 2024 v1.1, 2025 v1.1, 2026 v1.0, 2027 v1.0 (final, adds REHQR); layers CMS_#### rules + Schematron on STU 5.3
- CMS QRDA III IG for Eligible Clinicians — annual: 2024 v1.1, 2025 v1.1, 2026 v1.1; draft 2027 in comment (closes June 8 2026)
- QDM v5.6 (Quality Data Model) — data-element semantics underlying QRDA Cat I patient-data entries (PDF in Medgnosis repo root)
- CDA_SDTC.xsd — sdtc-namespace-enhanced CDA schema; QRDA files must validate against it or be rejected by HQR
- CMS_#### conformance rules — CMS-specific Schematron validation rules; e.g., 2025: removed CMS_0063, added CMS_0090 (facility closed), updated CMS_0082 ('2025C' EHR Certification ID format)
- Supplemental data code systems — CDC Race & Ethnicity (race/ethnicity), HL7 Administrative Gender (sex), Source of Payment Typology / SOP (payer)
- Core Clinical Data Elements (CCDEs) + claims-linking variables — Hybrid HWR/HWM Cat I payload (6 vitals + 7 labs for HWR)
- QPP JSON (Quality Payment Program scoring format) — target of QRDA III Conversion Tool; does NOT carry supplemental data/stratifications for MIPS
- HL7 FHIR cqf-measures Quality Measure IG v5.0.0 / STU5 (hl7.fhir.us.cqfmeasures#5.0.0, FHIR R4 / 4.0.1) — FHIR measure logic packaging (Clinical Reasoning + CQL)
- HL7 FHIR DEQM IG — Individual MeasureReport (replaces QRDA Cat I) and Summary MeasureReport (replaces QRDA Cat III); $evaluate-measure / $submit-data / $collect-data
- HL7 FHIR US Core + QI-Core (US Realm) — FHIR data-model profiles; QI-Core replaces QDM for FHIR dQMs
- HL7 FHIR Bulk Data Access ($export) — population-scale data feed for dQM evaluation
- CQL (Clinical Quality Language) — executable measure logic for both QDM-based eCQMs and FHIR dQMs

### Frontier & Trends

- CMS dQM transition is accelerating but still voluntary: draft CMS FHIR dQMs went to public comment (closed Feb 25, 2026) for EC, Hospital-Inpatient, and Hospital-Outpatient measures; DEQM MeasureReport is named the explicit successor to QRDA, but no hard cutover date has been set — QRDA/CDA remains the production mandate.
- Tool consolidation signals direction: MAT and Bonnie were retired into MADiE (June 28, 2024), which authors in both QDM and FHIR — CMS is funneling all measure development toward dual-model (and ultimately FHIR-native) artifacts.
- cqf-measures reaching STU5/v5.0.0 (published, not ballot) plus QI-Core maturing means the FHIR measure-logic + data-model stack is now stable enough to build production exporters against, not just prototype.
- Hybrid measures (HWR/HWM) becoming mandatory for IQR (FY2026) push hospitals toward EHR-extracted CCDE + claims linkage inside QRDA Cat I — a structural precursor to richer FHIR data exchange and a near-term must-have for hospital submitters.
- REHQR (Rural Emergency Hospital Quality Reporting) added to the 2027 CMS QRDA I IG — the program/measure surface keeps expanding, reinforcing that exporters must be version- and program-parameterized.
- Universal Foundation / measure-portfolio alignment: CMS continues to align measure sets across programs, increasing the value of a single computed-measure engine that can emit to multiple program channels (HQR Cat I, QPP Cat III, future DEQM).
- The QPP JSON conversion path remains a permanent intermediary for clinician programs (Cat III XML → QPP JSON via CMSgov/qpp-conversion-tool), with a separate Submission Validation API beyond conversion — two distinct validation surfaces vendors keep tripping over.

### Risks & Pitfalls

- Version drift: each program year ships a new CMS IG + Schematron + CMS_#### rule deltas (e.g., 2025 removed CMS_0063, added CMS_0090, changed CMS_0082 for '2025C' IDs). A hard-coded exporter silently rejects the next year. Exporter must be version-parameterized by reporting period.
- Conflating the two 2027 comment windows (as the draft did): 2027 Cat I closed March 25 2026 and is FINAL; only 2027 Cat III is still in draft (June 8 2026). Mis-tracking deadlines means building against the wrong/stale spec.
- CDA_SDTC.xsd hard requirement: files lacking the sdtc namespace enhancement are rejected outright by HQR — a single missing namespace breaks the whole submission.
- QPP JSON conversion gap: the CMSgov/qpp-conversion-tool drops supplemental data elements and reporting stratifications for MIPS. Relying on it blindly loses CMS-required strata; Medgnosis must inject those into QPP JSON itself where a program demands them.
- Two validation surfaces for QPP: passing the Conversion Tool is NOT the same as passing the QPP Submission Validation API (and MIPS-specific validations the sample files don't cover). Validate against both before claiming submittability.
- Hybrid measures are a separate payload: HWR/HWM require CCDE (6 vitals + 7 labs) plus claims-linking variables inside Cat I — a hand-rolled eCQM-only Cat I serializer will not satisfy these mandatory IQR measures.
- SQL-vs-CQL semantic divergence: Medgnosis computes measures in hand-authored SQL, not the canonical CQL data criteria (migration 052 explicitly warns 'authoritative roles come from the measure's CQL data criteria'). CVU+ Calculation Check will flag any numerator/denominator/exclusion logic that diverges from the certified measure logic — risking calculation rejections even when the XML is well-formed.
- FHIR readiness gap: Medgnosis FHIR is GET-only and unprofiled (no US Core/QI-Core, no MeasureReport, no $export). Entering the dQM lane requires profiling + new write/operation surfaces — a large, separate effort that cannot be deferred indefinitely as CMS pushes FHIR.
- Building twice: shipping a QRDA-only solution without a shared computed-measure → report-serialization abstraction risks a full rewrite when DEQM becomes mandatory. Architect the serializer layer to target both CDA QRDA and FHIR MeasureReport from the same fact tables.
- Supplemental-data completeness: every reported population must carry sex/race/ethnicity/payer in the correct code systems (CDC Race & Ethnicity, Administrative Gender, SOP). Missing or mis-coded supplemental data triggers warnings or rejections; Medgnosis strata are currently age/sex (migration 051) and lack race/ethnicity/payer dimensions.

## 5. Commercial CDS & Clinical-Knowledge Vendor Landscape

### Overview

The commercial clinical decision support (CDS) market is bifurcated into two tiers that rarely interoperate cleanly: (1) EHR-embedded, native CDS owned by the platform vendors — Epic, Oracle Health (Cerner), and MEDITECH — which control the clinical workflow surface (alerts, order sets, the order-entry path) and increasingly the predictive-model substrate; and (2) third-party clinical-knowledge and point-solution vendors that ride on top of the EHR — drug/disease reference (Wolters Kluwer UpToDate/Lexidrug/Medi-Span, Merative Micromedex, EBSCO DynaMedex, Elsevier ClinicalKey AI/ClinicalPath, FDB, IBM/Merative); order-set and care-plan content (Zynx Health, a Hearst Health company); workflow-CDS optimizers (Premier/Stanson Health); ambient documentation that increasingly feeds CDS (Microsoft Dragon Copilot, formerly Nuance DAX); and FDA-cleared diagnostic/imaging AI (Aidoc, Viz.ai, RapidAI, Qure.ai). The decisive competitive shift of 2025–2026 is generative-AI-grounded reference (UpToDate Expert AI launched to ~250K North American Enterprise users in Q4 2025, GA following; Elsevier ClinicalKey AI with paragraph-level citation traceability across 300+ hospitals — content/verification upgrades announced Feb 18, 2026 expanding to 130+ full-text journals; Merative Micromedex AI search Sept 2025; MEDITECH Expanse search/summarization on Google Vertex AI Search for Healthcare with Gemini) plus the rise of population-scale \"medical event foundation models.\" Epic Cosmos now spans ~304M patients / 20.7B encounters / 10B face-to-face visits across 2,133 hospitals, 48.8K clinics, ~591K physicians; Epic's Curiosity foundation-model family (arXiv 2508.12104, \"Generative Medical Event Models Improve with Scale\") was pretrained on 118M patients / 115B discrete medical events (151B tokens) from 310 health systems, and Epic's Comet platform (launched UGM Aug/Sept 2025) productizes predictive insights on that corpus — a real-world-data moat no startup can replicate.\n\n\"Best in class\" on the CDS dimension today is defined by four properties, all of which Medgnosis can credibly target as an open platform: (a) workflow-native delivery through standards (HL7 CDS Hooks 2.0.1 patient-view, order-select, order-sign; SMART on FHIR app launch; FHIR Clinical Reasoning / CDS-on-FHIR with CQL-expressed logic) rather than bolt-on portals; (b) demonstrable, externally-validated model performance with calibration — the single biggest credibility lever, given the Epic Sepsis Model debacle; (c) ruthless alert-fatigue management, because the entire interruptive drug-safety field is discredited by ~90% override rates; and (d) transparent, citation-grounded, governed AI rather than opaque proprietary scores. The incumbents are strong on data scale and EHR-surface ownership but structurally weak on transparency, external validation, alert burden, and standards-based portability — exactly the seam an open, OMOP/FHIR-native, \"logic-as-data\" platform can attack.\n\nThe most instructive cautionary tale is the Epic Sepsis Model (ESM). Wong et al. (JAMA Internal Medicine 2021, Univ. of Michigan, 38,455 hospitalizations) found the ESM had an AUC of only 0.63 versus Epic's marketed 0.76–0.83, with 33% sensitivity and a 12% positive predictive value, generating alerts on 18% of all hospitalized patients while missing two-thirds of sepsis cases — the textbook example of an unvalidated proprietary model deployed at scale without external benchmarking. The accompanying editorial (\"The Epic Sepsis Model Falls Short — The Importance of External Validation,\" PMID 34152360) crystallized the field's demand for independent, site-specific validation and calibration; Epic subsequently retrained/retired the v1 model. In deterioration/early-warning, the University of Chicago head-to-head (Churpek group; 362,926 ward encounters) ranked eCART (eCARTv5, ML) highest (AUROC 0.895), then NEWS2 (0.831), NEWS (0.829), Rothman Index (0.828), Epic Deterioration Index (0.808), and MEWS (0.757) — a key finding being that simpler, transparent scores (NEWS2) nearly match or beat sophisticated proprietary AI (EDI, RI), and proprietary complexity is not self-justifying.\n\nOn regulation and reimbursement: the PAMA Appropriate Use Criteria (AUC)/CDSM program for advanced imaging was PAUSED effective Jan 1, 2024 (regulations rescinded at 42 CFR 414.94 in the CY2024 PFS final rule; all claims edits removed for DOS on/after 1/1/2025; CMS no longer qualifies PLEs or CDSMs), so imaging-AUC CDS is no longer a CMS mandate — though Stanson Health/Elsevier still support it and statutory repeal requires Congress. FDA finalized the Predetermined Change Control Plan (PCCP) guidance Dec 4, 2024 (\"Marketing Submission Recommendations for a Predetermined Change Control Plan for AI-Enabled Device Software Functions\"), establishing the path for iterative AI/ML updates without new submissions; the Aug 2025 FDA/Health Canada/MHRA joint guiding principles followed. CMS's first AI-specific reimbursement pathway was the NTAP for Viz LVO computer-aided stroke triage, first granted Sept 2020 and renewed thereafter. The implication for Medgnosis is twofold: the SaMD/FDA boundary is the hard line (diagnostic AI is regulated; cohort analytics and care-gap CDS are generally not), and the standards layer (CDS Hooks, SMART, FHIR Clinical Reasoning/CQL, Da Vinci) is the durable, vendor-neutral moat to invest in.\n\nFor Medgnosis specifically: the codebase already has architectural pieces incumbents charge millions for and lock behind their EHR — a CDS Hooks 2.0.1 discovery endpoint (currently a single order-sign \"Care Gap Recommendations\" service at /cds-services; patient-view is not yet wired), an EAV \"logic-as-data\" rules engine with time-travel (as_of) and explain/transparency endpoints, VSAC value sets, FHIR R4 read APIs, and a 7-factor risk score. What separates it from \"best in class\" is exactly what incumbents do well: (1) externally-validated, calibrated predictive models (Medgnosis's risk score is a hand-weighted heuristic — fine for stratification but cannot be marketed as a sepsis/deterioration/readmission predictor without TRIPOD-AI / external-validation rigor); (2) a defensible alert-fatigue strategy (default to non-interruptive, tiered, patient-specific CDS, learning from FDB AlertSpace / Navigo and Stanson re-evaluation playbooks and the AHRQ/Sittig-Singh \"Five Rights\" + CDS Five Rights framework); and (3) citation-grounded generative reference to compete with UpToDate Expert AI / ClinicalKey AI / Micromedex AI — Medgnosis has Claude SDK + Ollama + Solr but no governed, traceable, evidence-cited answer engine yet. The strategic wedge: Medgnosis is open-source, OMOP-native (population-scale analytics the point-solution vendors lack), and standards-first, so it can be the transparent, portable, alert-fatigue-disciplined CDS layer that the proprietary incumbents structurally cannot be — provided it builds external-validation rigor and an alert-governance framework as first-class features.

### Key Findings

- **The Epic Sepsis Model is the field-defining cautionary tale: marketed AUC 0.76–0.83, real-world AUC 0.63** — Wong et al. external validation at University of Michigan (38,455 hospitalizations, Dec 2018–Oct 2019) found sensitivity 33%, specificity 83%, PPV 12%, NPV 95%, AUC 0.63 — versus Epic's marketed 0.76–0.83. The ESM generated alerts on ~18% of all hospitalized patients while failing to identify 67% of sepsis cases. This is the canonical example of a proprietary model deployed across hundreds of hospitals without independent external validation; Epic later released a retrained model and shifted toward customer-side validation tooling. Use it as Medgnosis's anti-pattern: never ship an unvalidated, uncalibrated proprietary score.
  - _Evidence:_ JAMA Internal Medicine 2021 (jamanetwork.com/journals/jamainternalmedicine/fullarticle/2781307); editorial PMID 34152360 'The Epic Sepsis Model Falls Short—The Importance of External Validation'; STAT/Michigan Medicine Health Lab coverage.
- **Simpler transparent early-warning scores rival or beat proprietary AI deterioration models (large head-to-head)** — University of Chicago (Churpek group) head-to-head across 362,926 ward encounters: eCART (eCARTv5, the only ML model) AUROC 0.895 > NEWS2 0.831 > NEWS 0.829 > Rothman Index 0.828 > Epic Deterioration Index (EDI) 0.808 > MEWS 0.757; outcome = ICU transfer or death within 24h. The open, transparent NEWS2 nearly matched the best AI and beat the proprietary Rothman Index and Epic's EDI, which had the lowest PPV. Proprietary complexity is not self-justifying. PulmCrit critique: the Rothman Index 'in isolation performs surprisingly well... but when added to usual clinical practice doesn't provide novel information or improve patient care.'
  - _Evidence:_ knowledge.uchicago.edu/record/13731; PMC11544488 'Early Warning Scores With and Without Artificial Intelligence'; insideprecisionmedicine.com 'Simple Beats Sophisticated in Early Warning Tools'; emcrit.org/pulmcrit/rothman-index

### Standards & Protocols

- HL7 CDS Hooks 2.0.1 — published spec (package hl7.fhir.uv.cds-hooks#2.0.1, FHIR R4 4.0.1) for user-facing remote CDS via hooks (patient-view, order-select, order-sign); discovery at {baseUrl}/cds-services.
- SMART on FHIR (SMART App Launch 2.x) — OAuth2-based EHR app launch/authorization; the mature path for embedding apps in the clinical workflow (1,000+ apps in galleries).
- FHIR R4 (4.0.1) — base interoperability resources (Patient, Condition, MedicationRequest, Observation, ServiceRequest) Medgnosis exposes for CDS read APIs.
- FHIR Clinical Reasoning / CDS-on-FHIR — PlanDefinition, ActivityDefinition, Library, Measure; lets CDS logic be authored as shareable FHIR resources.
- Clinical Quality Language (CQL) — the standard expression language for shareable, executable clinical logic and eCQMs; the standards-native alternative to a bespoke EAV rules engine.
- HL7 Da Vinci (CRD, DTR, PAS) — payer-side CDS over CDS Hooks for coverage requirements/prior auth.
- FDA PCCP final guidance (Dec 4, 2024) — 'Marketing Submission Recommendations for a Predetermined Change Control Plan for AI-Enabled Device Software Functions'; 3 components (Description of Modifications, Modification Protocol, Impact Assessment).
- FDA/Health Canada/MHRA Joint PCCP Guiding Principles (Aug 2025) — international harmonization of iterative-ML change control.
- PAMA AUC/CDSM program — PAUSED effective Jan 1, 2024; regulations rescinded at 42 CFR 414.94 (CY2024 PFS final rule); claims edits removed for DOS on/after 1/1/2025; statute not repealed.
- CMS NTAP (New Technology Add-on Payment) — first AI-specific reimbursement granted to Viz LVO (Sept 2020); precedent for AI device reimbursement.
- TRIPOD-AI (BMJ 2024) — transparent reporting standard for AI/ML clinical prediction models.
- DECIDE-AI (Nature Medicine 2022) — reporting guideline for early-stage clinical evaluation of decision-support AI.
- Arden Syntax (HL7) — legacy Medical Logic Module standard for sharable clinical knowledge; historical context for 'logic-as-data.'
- VSAC (NLM Value Set Authority Center) — authoritative value sets (already used by Medgnosis) for code-based cohort/measure logic.
- RxNorm / RxNav (NLM) — normalized drug nomenclature + open APIs for integrating drug logic without hand-authoring.
- AHRQ CDS Five Rights (Sittig/Osheroff) — right information, right person, right format, right channel, right time; the alert-governance framework.

### Frontier & Trends

- Population-scale medical-event foundation models (Epic Curiosity on 115B events; COMET Epic/Microsoft/Yale) shift CDS from hand-tuned scores to generative simulation of patient futures — but remain closed and EHR-locked.
- Generative, citation-grounded reference (UpToDate Expert AI, ClinicalKey AI per-paragraph traceability, Micromedex AI) is the 2025–2026 battleground; per-claim traceability + clinician-in-the-loop validation are the new table stakes.
- Ambient AI documentation (Dragon Copilot) is merging with CDS — generated notes now suggest orders inside Epic, making the ambient layer a new CDS trigger surface.
- Alert-fatigue remediation is shifting from severity-only filtering to patient-specific, tiered, non-interruptive CDS (FDB Navigo pattern) and peer-comparison/nudge-style delivery.
- Regulatory maturation of iterative ML: FDA PCCP + international guiding principles let models update post-clearance, but adoption is single-digit %; TRIPOD-AI/DECIDE-AI reporting and calibration (not just AUROC) are becoming credibility requirements.
- De-regulation of imaging AUC (PAMA pause) removes a CMS mandate and frees roadmap capacity, while NTAP precedents (Viz LVO) open AI reimbursement only for clearly-validated diagnostic devices.
- Standards convergence: CDS Hooks + CQL + FHIR Clinical Reasoning + Da Vinci are coalescing into a portable 'logic-as-shareable-FHIR' stack — the open counter-moat to proprietary EHR CDS.
- 'Simple beats sophisticated' evidence (NEWS2 vs EDI/Rothman) is pushing buyers toward transparent, externally-validated scores over opaque proprietary AI.

### Risks & Pitfalls

- Shipping Medgnosis's 7-factor risk score as a 'predictor' (sepsis/deterioration/readmission) without external validation + calibration would repeat the Epic Sepsis Model failure and destroy credibility; market it only as transparent stratification until TRIPOD-AI-grade validation exists.
- Interruptive alerts default to ~90% override; any care-gap CDS that fires as interruptive warnings will be ignored and breed clinician distrust — non-interruptive/tiered must be the default.
- Crossing the SaMD boundary (diagnostic/triage AI) pulls Medgnosis into 510(k)/De Novo/PCCP regulation — a different cost and timeline universe; keep scope to analytics/care-gap CDS unless deliberately pursuing a device.
- Hand-authoring drug-interaction/dosing/allergy logic competes against licensed oligopoly content (Medi-Span/FDB/Micromedex) Medgnosis cannot match for accuracy/liability — integrate RxNorm + a knowledge base instead.
- Ungoverned generative reference (Claude/Ollama) risks hallucination and untraceable citations — without per-claim traceability and a curated corpus it cannot compete with UpToDate Expert AI / ClinicalKey AI and could create clinical-safety/liability exposure.
- Cosmos-scale RWD is unattainable; trying to out-data Epic is a losing strategy — differentiate on openness, OMOP analytics, and standards portability instead.
- CDS Hooks adoption is still 'tentative' among EHR vendors due to developer workload; relying solely on hook firing may limit reach — pair with SMART on FHIR app launch and pull-based delivery.
- Building bespoke EAV rules logic instead of CQL risks non-portability and reinventing eCQM tooling; not adopting CQL/PlanDefinition forfeits the shareable-logic standards advantage.
- Calibration drift and dataset shift after deployment (esp. across sites) silently degrade any model — without monitoring (a PCCP-style protocol) performance decays unobserved.

### Implications for Medgnosis

- Build an external-validation + calibration harness as a first-class feature: per-score model card (population, AUROC + calibration-in-the-large/slope, decision-curve net benefit, subgroup performance), temporal/external validation reports, and a drift-monitoring job — the standards-aligned trust artifact (TRIPOD-AI/DECIDE-AI) incumbents rarely publish. Keep the 7-factor score labeled 'stratification heuristic,' not 'predictor,' until validated.
- Make alert governance a core engine capability: default CDS cards to non-interruptive (info), tier by severity, support per-site/per-user suppression (FDB AlertSpace pattern), patient-specific filtering (FDB Navigo pattern), and instrument override/acceptance telemetry. Encode the CDS Five Rights as configuration. Expose an alert-burden dashboard.
- Complete the CDS Hooks 2.0.1 surface: add patient-view and order-select services alongside the existing order-sign care-gaps service; ensure services fire cleanly on programmatically drafted orders (ambient/Dragon-Copilot-style) not just manual entry; publish a conformant discovery document and feedback endpoint.
- Adopt CQL + FHIR Clinical Reasoning (PlanDefinition/Library) as the authoring layer for shareable logic, with the EAV engine as the execution substrate — making Medgnosis logic portable and eCQM-aligned rather than bespoke-locked, while preserving the as_of/explain transparency endpoints.
- Ship a governed, citation-grounded answer engine: ground Claude/Ollama strictly on a curated corpus (own evidence + open guidelines + VSAC/RxNorm), enforce per-claim/per-paragraph citation traceability, add real-time verification + clinician-in-the-loop review, and refuse-on-low-confidence — to credibly sit beside UpToDate Expert AI / ClinicalKey AI without their corpus.
- Integrate licensed/open drug knowledge (RxNorm + RxNav/openFDA/DDInter, with an option to license Medi-Span/FDB) rather than hand-authoring drug-interaction/dosing/allergy logic; reserve Medgnosis-authored logic for population-health/care-gap rules where it is differentiated.
- Lean into the OMOP-native, population-scale wedge the point-solution vendors lack: cohort-level care-gap analytics, registry-style stratification, and pathway analysis surfaced into the same CDS Hooks/SMART surfaces — the analytics+CDS combination no single incumbent offers openly.
- De-prioritize any imaging-AUC/CDSM build (PAMA paused) and avoid the regulated diagnostic-SaMD lane entirely unless a deliberate, resourced 510(k)/De Novo+PCCP program is chosen; document the SaMD boundary in product scope so analytics CDS stays unregulated.
- Differentiate explicitly on openness/portability: publish the validation dossiers, the CQL logic, and the CDS Hooks conformance — turn transparency into the marketing wedge against opaque proprietary scores (EDI, Rothman, ESM).

## 6. Population-Health & Quality-Analytics Platform Competitors

### Overview

The population-health management (PHM), quality-analytics, and value-based-care (VBC) market splits into four tiers that together define Medgnosis's true peer set. Tier-1 enterprise PHM platforms — Arcadia, Innovaccer, Health Catalyst, and EHR-native Epic Healthy Planet/Cogito — win on three reinforcing assets: (1) normalized multi-source ingestion of clinical + claims + pharmacy + ADT + SDOH + RPM into a single longitudinal patient record (Arcadia's data lakehouse, Innovaccer's 100+ source integrations), (2) AI copilots that compress insight time (Innovaccer's Population Health Copilot 2.0, sub-60s), and (3) closed-loop, point-of-care gap closure with EHR write-back. Innovaccer's "Galaxy by Innovaccer" launch (Oct 14 2025) fused risk adjustment and HEDIS quality into one agentic, payer-facing system spanning both VBC and fee-for-service; it has since extended into Galaxy UM (utilization management / prior auth) using NLP+OCR with an "only auto-approve, never auto-deny" guardrail. Arcadia was named #1 in Healthcare Data Governance & Analytics in the 2025 Black Book Client Ratings, with clients citing its normalization of EHR, claims, SDoH, and RPM feeds.\n\nThe mid-market and safety-net tier is where an open, standards-first platform competes head-to-head. Azara Healthcare (DRVS) is Best in KLAS for PHM three consecutive years (2023, 2024, 2025) and merged with i2i Population Health in February 2025 under Insight Partners, unifying clinical + health-plan + HIE + SDOH data across community health centers, critical-access/rural hospitals, and IHS — a combined base impacting 50M+ patients, 1,000+ provider organizations, and dozens of payers, supporting 400+ measures across UDS, HEDIS, MIPS, and PCMH. (Correction: the draft's "Best in KLAS four consecutive years" overstates it — public sources confirm three: 2023–2025.) Lightbeam, Persivia CareSpace, Cedar Gate, Forward Health Group, and the standards-native Cozeva (Applied Research Works) round out this tier with risk-stratification + care-management workflows. Notably, Health Catalyst's 2025 KLAS PHM score (~75.0, vs a market average ~84.5) sits well below Best-in-KLAS, with KLAS flagging weak innovation — signaling that even well-capitalized incumbents have not locked the category; there is room for a transparent, standards-native challenger.\n\nThe quality/HEDIS and risk-adjustment specialist tier is built on certification and AI. NCQA HEDIS Measure Certification is the entry ticket: Inovalon's Converged Quality holds it for the 25th consecutive year (MY2025), one of only five long-standing certified vendors, processing >205M lives (~85% of all HEDIS-covered lives) for >100 plans. Critically, NCQA's digital path also matters: NCQA dQM specifications are authored in CQL+FHIR, and NCQA now offers implementation/engine-level certification plus a Data Aggregator Validation (DAV) program that — as of 2024 — validates FHIR output files conforming to US Core STU3 (v3.1.1), not just C-CDA. Risk adjustment has become an NLP-on-unstructured-text arms race spanning prospective/concurrent/retrospective workflows under the now-mandatory CMS-HCC V28 model (PY2025): Reveleer's EVE (Evidence Validation Engine) claims up to 99% accuracy identifying missed diagnoses (and now an explainable "EVE Hybrid AI" diagnosis-suspecting line on Google Cloud); Apixio was acquired by Datavant (Sept 2024), the Apixio brand sunset, and its capabilities relaunched as the Datavant Clinical Insights Platform (Mar 2025); Cotiviti's 25-year DxCG model (now DxCG Intelligence v6.0) anchors payer-side risk + quality and was reinforced by Cotiviti's acquisition of Edifecs (announced Feb 10 2025, completed Mar 31 2025). eCQM/QRDA submission vendors like Medisolv (ENCOR) handle the regulatory plumbing CMS requires — QRDA Cat I per-patient files, hybrid-measure CCDEs/Linking Variables, and now eCQM data-accuracy validation scoring (≥75% passing threshold beginning with CY2025 discharges, affecting FY2028 payment).\n\nThe entire market is migrating to a FHIR-native quality stack mandated by CMS's National Quality Strategy and Universal Foundation: measures authored in CQL over QI-Core v7.0.1 (STU7, aligned to US Core 7.0.0 / USCDI v4, which supersedes the QDM-based model), packaged as FHIR Measure/Library resources per the CQF Measures (cqf-measures) IG v5.0.0, evaluated via `$evaluate-measure`, exchanged as Da Vinci DEQM v5.0.0 (STU5, published May 2025) `$care-gaps` and Gaps-in-Care reports, with risk via the still-draft Da Vinci RA IG (v2.x, including draft digital Condition Category/dCC in CQL) and SDOH via Gravity SDOH-CC v2.3.0 (depends on US Core 3.1.1 / 6.1.0 / 7.0.0). CMS has FHIR specifications for a growing set of measures, calls FHIR "the standard for new measures," will annually raise the share of digital quality measures, and plans FHIR-receiving quality data system(s) by 2027.\n\nFor Medgnosis specifically: it already possesses the hardest-to-build asset the leaders monetize — a deep, normalized clinical EDW (1M+ patients, 28.7M encounters, 195M procedures) with a Kimball star, a 753-row measure library, 45 condition bundles with 354+ measures and dedup/prioritization logic, a logic-as-data CDS rules engine with time-travel and explainability, CDS Hooks 2.0, and a 1,545-value-set VSAC reference layer (ingested from the Parthenon VSAC store; codes are SNOMED-leaning per the EDW). Its decisive gaps are precisely the standards the market is consolidating on: no CQL engine (measures are bespoke SQL, uncertified and unsubmittable), no QRDA I/III or FHIR MeasureReport export, no `$evaluate-measure`/DEQM `$care-gaps`, no QI-Core conformance, no calibrated/ML or NLP-driven risk/RAF models, and no SDOH/Gravity capture. The strategic thesis is therefore inverted from a typical entrant: Medgnosis does not need to build the data platform the incumbents tout — it needs to wrap its existing engine in the FHIR-native, NCQA-certifiable, CMS-submittable standards layer, which would let an Apache-2.0 platform leapfrog closed incumbents on transparency, shareability, and certification credibility while matching them on workflow depth. The fastest credible standards beachhead is NCQA Data Aggregator Validation (US Core FHIR output) plus a CQL engine + DEQM `$care-gaps`, since those convert existing gap logic into the lingua franca payers and CMS already accept.

### Standards & Protocols

- HEDIS MY2025 (NCQA) — annual health-plan quality measure set; vendor Measure Certification is the trust gate.
- NCQA HEDIS Measure Certification — per-measure certification required to use NCQA measures for reporting/commercial products; Inovalon at 25 consecutive years (MY2025).
- NCQA dQM (digital quality measures) — NCQA measures expressed in CQL + FHIR; require NCQA implementation/engine certification to run.
- NCQA Data Aggregator Validation (DAV) — validates clinical-data output (C-CDA R2.1 and, since 2024, FHIR conforming to US Core STU3 v3.1.1) for quality/VBC/care-gap use; awards 'Validated Data Stream'.
- CQL (Clinical Quality Language, HL7) — computable measure-logic language; the universal authoring layer for eCQMs/dQMs.
- QI-Core IG v7.0.1 (STU7, FHIR R4 4.0.1) — data model profiles for quality, aligned to US Core 7.0.0 / USCDI v4; supersedes QDM for new measures (QDM v5.6 is the legacy model).
- CQF Measures IG (cqf-measures) v5.0.0 — FHIR Measure/Library packaging of CQL-based measures; defines computable/composite measure profiles and $evaluate-measure usage.
- $evaluate-measure (FHIR Clinical Reasoning) — operation that computes a MeasureReport (individual/subject-list/population) from a Measure + data.
- FHIR MeasureReport — standardized measure-result resource; replaces QRDA Cat I (individual) / Cat III (summary) in the FHIR path.
- Da Vinci DEQM IG v5.0.0 (STU5, published May 2025, FHIR R4) — $care-gaps operation + Gaps-in-Care Bundle/Composition; statuses open-gap|closed-gap|not-applicable|prospective.
- Da Vinci Risk Adjustment IG v2.x (draft/continuous build) — Risk Adjustment Coding Gap Report ($risk-gap), ra-measurereport, draft digital Condition Category (dCC) in CQL.
- Gravity Project SDOH-CC IG v2.3.0 (FHIR R4) — SDOH screening/diagnosis (ICD-10 Z55–Z65)/goals/interventions; depends on US Core 3.1.1, 6.1.0, 7.0.0.
- US Core IG (3.1.1 / 6.1.0 / 7.0.0) — base US-realm FHIR profiles aligned to USCDI v1/v3/v4; foundation for QI-Core, Gravity, and NCQA DAV FHIR validation.
- QRDA Cat I (individual, one file/patient) & Cat III (aggregate) — CMS eCQM submission formats for Hospital IQR/OQR/MIPS/APP.
- Hybrid measures — eCQM + claims linkage via Core Clinical Data Elements (CCDEs) and Linking Variables (e.g., Hybrid HWR/HWM).
- CMS eCQM data validation (FY2025 IPPS final rule, CMS-1808-F) — scored beginning CY2025 discharges (FY2028 payment); ≥75% validation threshold.
- CMS Universal Foundation / National Quality Strategy — aligned core measure set; FHIR-receiving quality data system(s) targeted by 2027; FHIR is 'the standard for new measures'.
- CMS-HCC Model V28 — fully phased/mandatory risk-adjustment model for payment year 2025; raises HCC-capture accuracy stakes.
- CDS Hooks 2.0 — point-of-care decision-support hook standard (Medgnosis already implements).
- MA Stars Health Equity Index — replaces the Reward Factor; rewards plans reducing disparities, linking SDOH measurement to MA payment.

### Frontier & Trends

- Convergence of risk + quality into one agentic platform — Innovaccer Galaxy (Oct 2025) and Cotiviti+Edifecs both collapse the historically separate HEDIS and RAF stacks into a single payer-facing system; expect provider-side equivalents next.
- Agentic AI replacing copilots — from <60s insight copilots to autonomous agents that orchestrate gap closure, prior auth (Galaxy UM), and coding review with human-in-the-loop guardrails ('only auto-approve, never auto-deny').
- FHIR-native digital quality is now the default — CMS, NCQA, and vendors (Cozeva's native FHIR dQM engine) are standardizing on CQL+FHIR Measure/Library/MeasureReport; QDM is legacy and QRDA is being supplemented by FHIR MeasureReport.
- Explainable, 'provable' risk suspecting — Reveleer's EVE Hybrid AI markets provability/explainability as a differentiator, signaling regulatory/RADV pressure favoring auditable NLP over black-box scoring (an area where Medgnosis's explainable rules engine is natively advantaged).
- Data-aggregator validation as a new credential — NCQA DAV extending to FHIR output creates a lower-friction certification on-ramp than full measure certification, ideal as a first standards beachhead.
- SDOH/health-equity moving from optional to reimbursement-linked — Gravity SDOH-CC v2.3.0 + MA Stars Health Equity Index + mandated SNP HRA questions make Z-code capture financially material.
- Record-linkage/exchange networks as the new moat — Datavant reframes risk tooling as 'clinical data infrastructure' built on a 50M+ record exchange network; ingestion breadth + linkage, not analytics, is the defensible asset.
- CMS hardening accuracy enforcement — eCQM validation scoring (≥75%, FY2028) and CMS-HCC V28 push the market from 'submit something' to 'submit accurately and defensibly', favoring auditable, standards-conformant engines.
- Open-source / Apache-2.0 positioning is differentiating — closed incumbents (~75 KLAS for Health Catalyst, KLAS flagging weak innovation) leave room for a transparent, shareable, certifiable platform.

### Risks & Pitfalls

- Standards-version drift: Da Vinci RA (v2.x) is still a draft/continuous build (not a published STU like DEQM 5.0.0); building to it risks churn — anchor first on DEQM $care-gaps and CQF Measures, treat RA/dCC as experimental.
- QI-Core v7 vs v7.0.1 confusion: v7.0.1 is the published STU7 (US Core 7.0.0 / USCDI v4); v7.0.0 exists mainly in continuous build — pin to 7.0.1 and the matching US Core to avoid profile mismatches.
- Certification is slow and expensive: NCQA Measure/implementation certification and DAV are multi-month, audited processes; a CQL engine alone does not confer HEDIS credibility.
- Bespoke-SQL lock-in: 753 SQL measures cannot be certified or submitted; re-authoring as CQL is non-trivial and must preserve the existing 30 dedup rules and bundle prioritization semantics or risk silent measure-result changes.
- VSAC currency/licensing: value sets are CMS-versioned per measurement year and require UMLS/VSAC licensing; the local 1,545-set snapshot (loaded from Parthenon) will drift from the authoritative MY release if not re-ingested.
- EDW codes are SNOMED-leaning, not ICD-10 — measures and Z-code/SDOH workflows must map across vocabularies; assuming claim-style ICD-10 will mis-fire on this clinical EDW.
- Single-org / read-only-FHIR ceiling: without claims/ADT/HIE ingestion and EHR write-back, Medgnosis cannot do the closed-loop, multi-source gap closure the Tier-1 leaders sell — analytics-only is a weaker market position.
- Risk-model credibility gap: a 7-factor 0–100 heuristic is not a calibrated/validated model (DxCG, HCC V28) and will not survive payer/RADV scrutiny; do not market it as risk adjustment.
- Overstating competitive parity: the draft's 'Azara four consecutive years' and the Galaxy 'multipoint gap closure' attribution are imprecise — keep competitive claims tightly sourced to avoid credibility loss in sales/grant contexts.
- Don't overbuild the data platform: the strategic error would be reinvesting in warehouse/ingestion the incumbents tout as moat while neglecting the cheaper, higher-leverage standards/certification wrapper.

## 7. AI/ML & Generative AI in CDS — Models, Safety & Regulation

### Overview

Clinical AI/ML has bifurcated into two distinct regulatory and technical universes that Medgnosis must navigate separately. The first is discriminative predictive modeling (readmission, sepsis, deterioration, mortality, no-show, disease-onset risk), which is mature, evidence-rich, and increasingly governed. The second is generative AI/LLMs (ambient documentation, RAG over guidelines, summarization, differential-diagnosis support), which is exploding in adoption but largely outside any cleared regulatory pathway. VERIFIED: as of end-of-2025 the FDA had authorized 1,451 AI-enabled devices (1,104 / 76% radiology cumulative; 75% of 2025 authorizations; GE HealthCare the leading single submitter), and NONE used adaptive (continuously-learning) or generative AI — every clearance to date is a "locked" model. The dominant cautionary tale is the Epic Sepsis Model: Wong et al. (JAMA Internal Medicine, June 21, 2021; 27,697 patients / 38,455 hospitalizations at Michigan Medicine, Dec 2018–Oct 2019) externally validated it at AUC 0.63 versus Epic's claimed 0.76–0.83, sensitivity 33%, specificity 83%, PPV 12%, NPV 95%, generating an alert on 18% of all hospitalized patients and a number-needed-to-evaluate of ~109 alerts per additional sepsis case — a textbook coupling of poor external validity AND alert fatigue. This is the canonical evidence that vendor-reported, internally-validated performance cannot be trusted and that local validation plus calibration monitoring is mandatory.\n\nThe regulatory architecture has FOUR load-bearing pillars (the draft had three; the EU AI Act is the missing fourth). (1) FDA's Clinical Decision Support Software final guidance (Sept 28, 2022) operationalizes the 21st Century Cures Act §3060 four-criterion test separating exempt Non-Device CDS from regulated SaMD; the pivotal criteria are #3 (recommendations/options, not a single directive) and #4 (the HCP can independently review the basis so they need not rely primarily on the software). VERIFIED CORRECTION: FDA published a REVISED CDS final guidance on January 6, 2026 (announced by Commissioner Makary at CES), now exercising enforcement discretion for SINGLE recommendations where only one option is "clinically appropriate" (relaxing the 2022 multiple-options requirement) — but it is conspicuously SILENT on generative AI/chatbots, leaving genAI CDS unaddressed. (2) ONC/ASTP's HTI-1 final rule (published Jan 9, 2024) created the Decision Support Interventions (DSI) certification criterion at 45 CFR 170.315(b)(11), defining "Predictive DSI" and mandating "source attributes" — the de-facto "nutrition label" — 31 source attributes for predictive DSIs and 13 for evidence-based DSIs, plus Intervention Risk Management (IRM) practices; certified-IT update deadline was Dec 31, 2024 with ongoing maintenance from Jan 1, 2025. (3) The lifecycle/adaptive-AI layer: FDA's Predetermined Change Control Plan (PCCP) final guidance (published Dec 3, 2024) lets devices update without new submissions and now covers ALL AI-enabled device software functions (not just ML), plus the FDA/Health Canada/MHRA Good Machine Learning Practice (GMLP) 10 guiding principles (Oct 2021) and the companion 5 PCCP principles. (4) The EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689): AI used for diagnosis, clinical decision support, triage, and monitoring is HIGH-RISK; for AI that is itself a regulated medical device under MDR/IVDR the high-risk obligations apply from Aug 2027 (general high-risk from Aug 2, 2026), layered on top of MDR conformity assessment — a dual-regulation burden a likely "Digital Omnibus" may yet delay.\n\nTrustworthy-AI methodology has consolidated around named, citable standards: TRIPOD+AI (BMJ 2024;385:e078378, published April 16, 2024; Collins, Moons, Dhiman, Riley, Beam, Van Calster) for transparent reporting including fairness and calibration; the companion TRIPOD-LLM (Nature Medicine 2025) for LLM-specific studies; CHAI (Coalition for Health AI) Model Cards and Assurance Lab certification with a public Model Card Registry launched Feb 28, 2025 (Providence, Cleveland Clinic, Kaiser Permanente and ~11 founding systems); the WHO "Ethics and governance of AI for health: large multi-modal models" guidance (Jan 18, 2024; 6 principles, 40+ recommendations); NIST AI RMF 1.0 (Jan 2023) and its Generative AI Profile (NIST AI 600-1, July 2024); Finlayson et al.'s NEJM 2021 "Clinician and Dataset Shift" framing of silent failure; and a deep alert-fatigue evidence base (medication CDS override rates 49–96%, DDI overrides ~90% even at highest severity, only ~26% of overrides judged appropriate). Generative AI adds a new evaluation discipline — MedHELM, MedHallu/MedHallBench hallucination benchmarks, npj Digital Medicine summarization-safety frameworks — alongside ambient-documentation RCT evidence (NEJM AI 2024–25, 238 physicians, 14 specialties) showing DAX Copilot / Nabla / Abridge reduce documentation time and burnout. The ambient-scribe market crossed ~$1.5B in 2025 (Nuance/Microsoft ~33%, Abridge ~30% at a $5.3B valuation, Ambience ~13%, Suki ~10%, Nabla), and Epic's own native scribe + predictive suite (Cosmos, Best Care Choices, ~150 prediction models) is now the incumbent platform threat.\n\nFor Medgnosis specifically, this stream is the single largest strategic gap and the single largest differentiation opportunity. Medgnosis today has ZERO predictive/ML models — its risk stratification is an explicit hand-weighted 7-factor heuristic (apps/api/src/services/riskScoring.ts: F01 age max 20, F02 conditions 25, F03 vitals 20, F04 labs 20, F05 care gaps 15, F06 encounters 15, F07 meds 15; raw max 130 capped to 0–100; bands low<25/moderate>=25/high>=50/critical>=75; every factor's weight+contribution+detail persisted to patient_risk_history) — plus an EAV "logic-as-data" clinical-rule engine and a Claude/Ollama-backed AI encounter scribe with provenance. Crucially, this architecture is a near-perfect substrate for leading on transparency: the rules engine already has explain/transparency endpoints and time-travel (as_of), the scribe already tracks provenance, the risk service already emits per-factor attribution, and CDS Hooks 2.0 is wired. The decisive move is NOT to chase predictive accuracy first; it is to build the GOVERNANCE LAYER the entire industry is being forced toward — a native HTI-1 DSI "nutrition label" / CHAI-Model-Card registry, TRIPOD+AI-conformant model documentation, calibration/drift monitoring, fairness audits, and an alert-effectiveness/override-rate analytics loop — and to deliberately architect every CDS output to satisfy FDA Non-Device CDS Criterion #4 (source-of-basis transparency) so Medgnosis remains exempt SaMD by design. An open-source platform that ships the transparency-and-monitoring scaffolding most vendors treat as an afterthought can credibly claim leadership on responsible clinical AI.

### Key Findings

- **FDA's CDS four-criterion test is the gate between exempt CDS and regulated SaMD — and Criterion #4 ('independently review the basis') is the transparency requirement that defines responsible CDS architecture; the Jan 6, 2026 revision relaxed the single-recommendation rule but stayed silent on genAI** — Per the Sept 28, 2022 final guidance, software is Non-Device (exempt) CDS only if it meets ALL four criteria: (1) does NOT acquire/process/analyze medical images, signals, or patterns from a signal-acquisition system; (2) displays/analyzes/prints medical information normally communicated between HCPs; (3) provides recommendations (information/options) rather than a specific directive; (4) is intended to enable the HCP to independently review the basis so they do not rely primarily on the recommendation. CORRECTION/UPDATE: FDA published the revised CDS final guidance on January 6, 2026 (not just 'January 2026'); it introduces limited enforcement discretion for a SINGLE output where only one option is 'clinically appropriate' (term left undefined), relocates the time-critical limitation, and — despite being marketed as AI-focused — provides NO substantive position on generative AI or chatbots. FDA continues to assert authority over opaque models, time-critical tools, and software that substitutes for clinical judgment.
  - _Evidence:_ FDA 'Clinical Decision Support Software' final guidance Sept 28 2022 & revised Jan 6 2026; Covington & Burling '5 Key Takeaways' (Jan 2026); Ropes & Gray, Faegre Drinker, Arnold & Porter, Orrick, Nixon Law Group, ACR client alerts (Jan 2026).
- **ONC/ASTP HTI-1 created the Predictive DSI 'nutrition label' — 31 source attributes plus mandatory Intervention Risk Management — and it certifies the EHR, not the model** — HTI-1 final rule (published Jan 9, 2024; DSI criterion at 45 CFR 170.315(b)(11)) defines 'Predictive DSI' as technology supporting decisions based on models that derive relationships from training data to produce a prediction, classification, recommendation, evaluation, or analysis. Certified Health IT must surface source attributes: 31 for Predictive DSIs (categories: Details/Output, Purpose, Cautioned/Out-of-Scope Use, Development Details & Input Features, Fairness/bias process, External Validation, Quantitative Performance/validity, Ongoing Maintenance/monitoring, Update schedule) and 13 for Evidence-based DSIs. Developers must implement IRM practices: risk ANALYSIS (validity, reliability, robustness, fairness, intelligibility, safety, security, privacy), risk MITIGATION, and GOVERNANCE (data acquisition/management/use). CORRECTION: certified-IT update deadline was Dec 31, 2024; ongoing-maintenance and IRM-summary obligations begin Jan 1, 2025 (the draft's 'compliance required by Jan 1, 2025' conflated these). Source attributes must be plain-language to help users assess whether a Predictive DSI is FAVES (Fair, Appropriate, Valid, Effective, Safe).
  - _Evidence:_ ONC HTI-1 DSI Fact Sheet (healthit.gov 2023-12); 170.315(b)(11) test method (healthit.gov/test-method/decision-support-interventions); Mintz (Jan 8 2024); Crowell Health Solutions (May 2024); AHIMA AI Regulatory Resource Guide.
- **The Epic Sepsis Model external-validation failure is the field's defining proof that internal/vendor performance does not transfer — poor discrimination + crushing alert burden + miscalibration** — Wong et al. (JAMA Intern Med, June 21, 2021; 27,697 patients / 38,455 hospitalizations at Michigan Medicine, Dec 6 2018–Oct 20 2019; ESM scored every 15 min) found AUC 0.63 (vs Epic-reported 0.76–0.83), sensitivity 33%, specificity 83%, PPV 12%, NPV 95%; the model alerted on 18% of all hospitalized patients, and clinicians would evaluate ~109 alerts per additional true sepsis case, identifying only ~7% of sepsis cases missed by clinicians. The companion editorial 'The Epic Sepsis Model Falls Short — The Importance of External Validation' (Habib, Lin, Grant) made external validation the central lesson. Epic subsequently moved to a retrainable/recalibrated model. This is the load-bearing citation for mandating LOCAL validation, calibration assessment (not just AUC), and silent-period shadow deployment before any model fires alerts.
  - _Evidence:_ Wong A, Otles E, Donnelly JP, et al. JAMA Intern Med 2021;181(8):1065-1070 (jamanetwork.com/.../2781307); editorial PMID 34152360. AUC/sensitivity/specificity/PPV/NPV and 18%-alert / 109-NNE figures confirmed via JAMA + Infectious Disease Advisor + JAMIA Open replication (ooae133).
- **Generative AI is in mass clinical use for ambient documentation with RCT-grade efficiency evidence, yet sits entirely outside FDA's cleared-device universe — a >$1.5B market with no clearances** — Ambient AI scribes (Microsoft/Nuance DAX Copilot, Abridge, Suki, Ambience, Nabla) are the breakout genAI clinical use case. A NEJM AI randomized study (data Nov 2024–Jan 2025; 238 outpatient physicians, 14 specialties) found DAX Copilot and Nabla reduced documentation time and burnout vs usual care. Market data (2025): ambient-scribe market crossed ~$1.5B growing ~25%/yr; Microsoft/Nuance ~33% share, Abridge ~30% (raised $773M total; $300M Series E June 2025 at $5.3B valuation), Ambience ~13% (unicorn, $243M Series C), Suki ~10% ($168M total), Nabla ($70M raise June 2025, ~$120M total). KLAS: ~93% of health systems project moderate-to-deep ambient adoption within 6 months. Pricing: DAX $400–600/mo/physician, Abridge ~$250, Nabla ~$119. CRITICAL: FDA's first Digital Health Advisory Committee meeting (Nov 20–21, 2024) on Total Product Lifecycle for GenAI devices confirmed ZERO authorized devices use generative/adaptive AI, and the Jan 2026 CDS revision offered no genAI position — leaving ambient scribes, RAG assistants, and chatbots in a governance vacuum that platforms must self-fill. Epic now ships a native scribe, threatening third-party vendors.
  - _Evidence:_ NEJM AI 2024-25 (AIoa2400659); FDA DHAC meeting Nov 2024 (Federal Register 2024-21074); STAT (Nabla $70M, Jun 17 2025); Sacra (Abridge); Becker's ambient market-share; Simbo AI pricing; The Imaging Wire (Dec 2025 device count).
- **Trustworthy-AI reporting has consolidated around TRIPOD+AI, TRIPOD-LLM, and CHAI Model Cards as the de-facto documentation standards that operationalize HTI-1** — TRIPOD+AI (BMJ 2024;385:e078378, published April 16, 2024; Collins, Moons, Dhiman, Riley, Beam, Van Calster) updates the 2015 TRIPOD reporting standard to cover both regression and ML prediction models, adding fairness, open science, patient/public involvement, and explicit calibration reporting (27-item checklist). TRIPOD-LLM (Gallifant et al., Nature Medicine 2025) extends this to LLM studies. CHAI (Coalition for Health AI) advanced draft Assurance Lab certification and 'nutrition label' Model Cards (Oct 2024) and launched a public Model Card Registry on Feb 28, 2025 with founding systems including Providence, Cleveland Clinic, Kaiser Permanente, Memorial Sloan Kettering, Mayo-adjacent Mercy, Mount Sinai, Rush, Sharp, Stanford Medicine, UMass Memorial, UT Health — explicitly aligned to HTI-1's required attributes (funding source, intended use, intended population, known risks, performance). These are the templates Medgnosis should emit natively rather than invent.
  - _Evidence:_ Collins GS et al. TRIPOD+AI, BMJ 2024;385:e078378 (DOI 10.1136/bmj-2023-078378); Gallifant J et al. TRIPOD-LLM, Nat Med 2025; CHAI PRNewswire (Oct 2024); Healthcare Dive / Fierce Healthcare (Model Card Registry, Feb 28 2025).
- **Dataset shift causes silent clinical-model failure; calibration drift, not just discrimination, is the monitoring target** — Finlayson, Subbaswamy, Singh et al. ('The Clinician and Dataset Shift in Artificial Intelligence', NEJM 2021;385:283-286) established that models silently degrade when deployment data diverges from training data — from EHR upgrades, coding changes (e.g., ICD transitions), practice shifts, or population change — and recommended multidisciplinary governance committees, ongoing monitoring partnerships, and frontline mechanisms to flag suspected shift. The implication: production clinical ML requires continuous calibration monitoring (calibration-in-the-large, calibration slope, ECE), discrimination tracking (AUC/AUPRC over time), input-distribution/covariate-shift detection, and outcome surveillance — an MLOps discipline most CDS platforms lack. CHAI's 2025 post-deployment monitoring workstream directly operationalizes this.
  - _Evidence:_ Finlayson SG et al. NEJM 2021;385:283-286 (PMID 34260843, DOI 10.1056/NEJMc2104626); CHAI post-deployment monitoring initiative (Fierce Healthcare 2025).
- **Alert fatigue is quantified and devastating — override rates of 49–96% — making alert EFFECTIVENESS analytics a first-class platform requirement, not an afterthought** — Medication-related CDS override rates range 49–96%; drug-drug interaction (DDI) alerts are overridden ~90% even at highest severity, and only ~26% of overrides were judged clinically appropriate in systematic review. Excess low-specificity alerting both desensitizes clinicians and (when justified overrides mask real risk) is associated with adverse events. The Epic Sepsis Model's 18%-of-patients-alerted / 109-NNE is the predictive-model analog. This convergence is why HTI-1 IRM, FDA Criterion #4, CHAI, and the ONC 'Five Rights of CDS' (right info, person, format, channel, time) all demand transparency + tunability + measured effectiveness — and why Medgnosis must instrument override rates, alert-to-action conversion, acceptance per rule, and alert volume per clinician from day one.
  - _Evidence:_ JAMIA medication-CDS override studies; systematic review of overridden-alert appropriateness (PMC7400042); JMIR Med Inform ML alert-fatigue model (2020); ONC 'Five Rights of CDS' (healthit.gov).
- **PCCP + GMLP define the adaptive-AI lifecycle path any future Medgnosis learning model must follow; PCCP now covers ALL AI device functions, not just ML** — FDA's PCCP final guidance (published Dec 3, 2024: 'Marketing Submission Recommendations for a Predetermined Change Control Plan for Artificial Intelligence-Enabled Device Software Functions') lets manufacturers pre-specify allowed modifications, validation protocols, and impact assessments so models update without new 510(k)/De Novo/PMA submissions. CORRECTION: unlike the draft guidance (ML-only), the FINAL version applies to ALL AI-enabled device software functions and added intended-population (race/ethnicity, sex, age, disease severity) and labeling-update recommendations. The FDA/Health Canada/MHRA GMLP 10 Guiding Principles (Oct 2021) — multidisciplinary expertise, good software engineering & data management, representative/independent train-test data, transparency to users, monitored deployment with managed re-training risk — plus the companion 5 PCCP guiding principles are the design contract for any continuously-learning model.
  - _Evidence:_ FDA PCCP final guidance (fda.gov, Dec 3 2024); Ropes & Gray, McDermott+, Foley & Lardner, King & Spalding analyses (Dec 2024–Jan 2025); FDA/Health Canada/MHRA GMLP 10 principles (Oct 2021, fda.gov).
- **The EU AI Act adds a second, MDR-layered high-risk regime that the draft omitted entirely — clinical AI is high-risk by definition** — EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, in force Aug 1 2024) classifies AI for diagnosis, clinical decision support, treatment recommendation, triage, and patient monitoring as HIGH-RISK (Article 6 / Annex III + Annex I medical-device coupling). High-risk obligations include risk management, data governance, technical documentation, logging, human oversight, transparency, accuracy/robustness/cybersecurity, and post-market monitoring — layered ON TOP of MDR/IVDR conformity assessment (notified-body involvement). Timeline: prohibited-practice bans Feb 2 2025; GPAI obligations Aug 2 2025; general high-risk Aug 2 2026; AI that is a regulated medical device under Annex I gets an extended runway to Aug 2 2027. A proposed 'Digital Omnibus on AI' (announced 2025) is expected to postpone several deadlines given slow national-authority designation and missing harmonized standards. Any Medgnosis EU deployment must satisfy both AI Act high-risk Annex IV technical documentation AND MDR — the AI Act technical file substantially overlaps the HTI-1 source attributes Medgnosis should already emit.
  - _Evidence:_ Regulation (EU) 2024/1689; DQS Global 'AI Act & AI-Enabled Medical Devices: Regulatory Status 2026'; Frontiers in Digital Health (2026.1808373); Tandem Health EU AI Act healthcare explainer; Gardner Law compliance timeline.
- **WHO LMM guidance and NIST AI RMF + Generative AI Profile are the international/non-binding governance frameworks Medgnosis should map its controls to** — WHO 'Ethics and governance of AI for health: Guidance on large multi-modal models' (Jan 18, 2024) sets 6 ethical principles (protect autonomy; promote well-being/safety; transparency & explainability; accountability; inclusiveness & equity; sustainability) and 40+ recommendations specifically for generative LMMs, flagging fabrication/bias, poor training-data quality, and automation bias. NIST AI RMF 1.0 (Jan 26, 2023) defines the GOVERN/MAP/MEASURE/MANAGE functions, and the companion Generative AI Profile (NIST AI 600-1, July 26, 2024) enumerates 12 GenAI-specific risks (confabulation, dangerous/violent content, data privacy, harmful bias, info integrity, etc.) with mitigations. Neither is binding, but both are the vocabulary procurement teams and assurance labs use; emitting a control matrix mapped to NIST AI RMF + WHO + HTI-1 IRM is cheap differentiation.
  - _Evidence:_ WHO 'Ethics and governance of AI for health: large multi-modal models' (who.int/publications/i/item/9789240084759, Jan 18 2024); NIST AI RMF 1.0 (NIST AI 100-1); NIST AI 600-1 Generative AI Profile (July 2024).
- **LLM clinical-evaluation tooling has matured into named benchmarks Medgnosis can adopt rather than build — MedHELM, MedHallu, hallucination/faithfulness metrics** — Generative CDS demands a distinct evaluation discipline. Stanford CRFM's MedHELM (2025) provides a holistic clinical-task benchmark across real clinician-defined tasks; MedHallu and MedHallBench score medical hallucination; npj Digital Medicine frameworks address summarization safety and omission/fabrication in clinical text. For RAG-over-guidelines (a natural Medgnosis fit given its rules engine + provenance), faithfulness/groundedness metrics (answer attributable to retrieved source) and citation-accuracy checks are the relevant guardrails — and directly satisfy FDA Criterion #4 by forcing every generated recommendation to expose its retrieved basis. ELEVATE-GenAI and emerging LLM reporting checklists complement TRIPOD-LLM for study transparency.
  - _Evidence:_ Stanford CRFM MedHELM (2025); MedHallu / MedHallBench benchmark papers (2024-25); npj Digital Medicine clinical-summarization safety frameworks; ELEVATE-GenAI (arXiv 2501.12394).

### Frontier & Trends

- Regulatory deregulation in the US (Jan 6 2026 CDS revision relaxes single-recommendation rule) while the EU tightens (AI Act high-risk) — platforms need a jurisdiction-aware governance layer, not one-size-fits-all.
- GenAI remains in a deliberate FDA blind spot: zero generative/adaptive clearances; DHAC TPLC framework pending; ambient scribes and RAG assistants self-governing via CHAI/NIST until rules arrive.
- Post-deployment monitoring is the next battleground: CHAI's monitoring workstream + Finlayson dataset-shift thinking are pushing the field from 'validate once' to continuous calibration/drift surveillance as a product feature.
- 'Nutrition label' convergence: HTI-1 source attributes, CHAI Model Cards, EU AI Act Annex IV, and TRIPOD+AI are collapsing into one overlapping documentation artifact — emit once, satisfy many.
- Ambient documentation is commoditizing (>$1.5B market, price compression to ~$119/mo, Epic shipping native) — differentiation moves to integrated, governed, source-attributed CDS rather than raw transcription.
- RAG-over-guidelines with mandatory citation/faithfulness is becoming the safe pattern for generative CDS because grounded, reviewable output directly satisfies FDA Criterion #4.
- LLM-as-judge and named clinical benchmarks (MedHELM, MedHallu) are turning genAI safety into a measurable release gate rather than a qualitative claim.
- Fairness/calibration auditing is shifting left into CI: subgroup calibration and equal-opportunity gaps computed automatically at model registration, not in a one-off paper.
- Agentic clinical AI (tool-using assistants that take actions) is emerging just as regulators worry about software 'substituting for clinical judgment' — raises the Criterion-#4 reliance bar.
- Open-source responsible-AI tooling is a credible wedge: most commercial vendors treat transparency/monitoring as an afterthought, leaving room for a platform that ships it as scaffolding.

### Risks & Pitfalls

- Trusting vendor/internal performance: the Epic Sepsis Model proves internally-reported AUC (0.76–0.83) can collapse externally (0.63); never deploy a model without LOCAL validation and calibration assessment.
- Optimizing discrimination (AUC) while ignoring calibration: a well-discriminating but miscalibrated model produces systematically wrong risk magnitudes and silent dataset-shift failure.
- Alert fatigue: low-specificity alerting (49–96% override rates; ESM alerting 18% of patients) desensitizes clinicians and can mask real risk — must instrument override/acceptance from day one.
- Drifting out of the Non-Device CDS exemption: processing an image/signal (Criterion #1) or issuing a non-reviewable directive in a time-critical setting (Criterion #4) silently converts Medgnosis into a regulated device.
- Generative-AI governance vacuum: shipping an ambient scribe or RAG assistant with no hallucination eval, no faithfulness/citation guardrail, and no provenance invites both clinical harm and future regulatory exposure.
- Ignoring the EU AI Act: clinical CDS is high-risk by definition; a US-only compliance posture blocks EU deployment and misses the overlapping documentation reuse.
- HTI-1 misread: the criterion certifies the EHR/IT and demands plain-language source attributes — technical model cards alone do not satisfy it; the maintenance/IRM clock started Jan 1 2025.
- Hand-weighted heuristic masquerading as 'AI': Medgnosis's 7-factor score is defensible and transparent but must be documented and labeled as a rule-based score, not a validated predictive model, to avoid overclaiming.
- Adaptive/learning models without a PCCP: any future continuously-learning Medgnosis model that updates in production without a predetermined change-control plan is ungovernable and non-marketable as a device.
- Automation bias and over-reliance: even transparent CDS can erode independent clinical judgment; UI must preserve reviewability and avoid presenting outputs as directives.
- Fairness blind spots: failing to report subgroup calibration/performance (TRIPOD+AI, HTI-1 fairness attribute, NIST harmful-bias) is now both an ethics and a compliance gap.
- Provenance gaps in genAI output: if generated text cannot be traced to its source evidence, it fails Criterion #4, CHAI, WHO transparency, and clinician trust simultaneously.

## 8. Regulatory & Interoperability Frontier — ONC, CMS-0057-F, Da Vinci, TEFCA

### Overview

The US regulatory frontier for a clinical analytics/CDS platform is defined by three converging forces: (1) ONC/ASTP certification policy, anchored by HTI-1 (final, published Jan 9, 2024; effective in stages through 2024-2026), which created the Decision Support Interventions (DSI) criterion at 45 CFR 170.315(b)(11) requiring source-attribute transparency for BOTH evidence-based DSIs (13 source attributes) and Predictive DSIs (31 source attributes), plus Predictive DSI Intervention Risk Management (IRM) summary disclosure, USCDI as the certified content baseline, and real-world testing; (2) CMS payer interoperability, anchored by CMS-0057-F (final Jan 17, 2024), which mandates four production FHIR APIs — Patient Access, Provider Access, Payer-to-Payer, and Prior Authorization — by January 1, 2027, with prior-auth turnaround/specific-denial-reason/public-metrics changes starting January 1, 2026; and (3) the HL7 Da Vinci + digital-quality stack (CRD/DTR/PAS, DEQM, cqf-measures, PDex, Risk Adjustment, Member Attribution) that operationalizes these mandates over FHIR.\n\nA critical 2025-2026 development reshapes the certification picture. CORRECTION TO DRAFT: ONC/ASTP did NOT pursue HTI-2 to finalization on its major content items — it WITHDREW the non-finalized HTI-2 proposals (Federal Register doc 2025-23890, published Dec 29, 2025, effective Dec 29, 2025), including USCDI v4 adoption, the USCDI v3 expiration date, the FIPS 140-2 Annex A encryption update, and other items. In the same Dec 29, 2025 Federal Register issue, ASTP/ONC proposed HTI-5 (FR doc 2025-23896; the rule was placed on public inspection/signed ~Dec 22, 2025), a deregulatory rule that proposes to REMOVE 34 and UPDATE 7 of the program's ~60 certification criteria — including removing all §170.315(d) privacy/security criteria (citing HIPAA Security Rule overlap) and descoping the HTI-1 'Insights' Condition — while explicitly RETAINING the §170.315(g)(10) Standardized FHIR API criterion as the program's API-first backbone. ASTP/ONC estimates the HTI-5 cuts save certified developers up to ~4,000 hours each in year one (the draft's '~1.4M industry compliance hours' figure is unverified and has been replaced with this confirmed per-developer estimate). HTI-5 comments are due Feb 27, 2026. The strategic signal is unambiguous: the federal floor is collapsing toward FHIR APIs, SMART/OAuth, USCDI content, and Bulk FHIR — and away from prescriptive functional criteria.\n\nOn the quality side, CMS is consolidating on the 'Universal Foundation' of aligned measures (NEJM 2023) across MSSP, MIPS/MVPs, MA Star Ratings, and Medicaid Core Sets, implementing those measures by ~2026 and standing up FHIR-receiving quality data systems by ~2027. The technical realization is the cqf-measures (Quality Measure) IG v5.0.0 (FHIR Measure + Library replacing HQMF, CQL logic, $evaluate-measure → MeasureReport), QI-Core (currently v6.0.0, built on US Core) as the data model, and DEQM v5.0.0 / STU5 (package generated 2025-05-19) individual/summary reporting that is explicitly positioned to replace QRDA Category I and III. CMS opened a public comment period on draft CMS FHIR dQMs — 49 Eligible Clinician + 17 Hospital Inpatient + 4 Hospital Outpatient measures (70 total) — closing Feb 25, 2026 via the ONC Jira tracker. On the exchange side, TEFCA is live with ELEVEN designated QHINs as of mid-2026 (CommonWell, eClinicalWorks/PrismaNet, eHealth Exchange, Epic Nexus, Health Gorilla, Kno2, KONZA, MedAllies, Netsmart, Oracle Health Information Network, Surescripts/Interconnect) — the draft undercounted at ~10 and omitted Netsmart, Oracle, and Surescripts — with a FHIR/Bulk roadmap, and Carequality/CommonWell converging toward it. Risk adjustment has fully transitioned to CMS-HCC V28 (100% V28 for PY2026), with CY2026 also using 100% updated RxHCC and the updated ESRD models. SDOH is standardizing through the Gravity Project SDOH Clinical Care FHIR IG, ICD-10-CM Z-codes (Z55-Z65), LOINC, SNOMED CT, and USCDI's SDOH data class. Information blocking now carries real penalties: up to $1M per violation for developers/HINs/HIEs (OIG CMP, effective Sept 1, 2023) and Medicare-payment disincentives for providers (effective Jul 31, 2024; MSSP Jan 1, 2025) — and in Sept/Oct 2025 HHS-OIG and ASTP jointly issued an Information Blocking Enforcement Alert signaling active enforcement.\n\nFor Medgnosis specifically, this stream both validates the architecture and exposes the gaps. Medgnosis's strengths map unusually well to the frontier: its EAV 'logic-as-data' rules engine (rulesEngine.ts) with explain/transparency endpoints and time-travel is a natural fit for HTI-1 DSI source-attribute disclosure (13 evidence-based / 31 predictive) and Predictive DSI IRM; its CDS Hooks 2.0 (released Aug 2022) discovery + order-sign services (routes/cds-hooks) are structurally the same substrate Da Vinci CRD needs; and its 45-bundle, 354+-measure care-gap engine is a near-perfect DEQM Gaps in Care producer/responder. Medgnosis already has measureEvaluator.ts / measureCalculatorV2.ts, hccAnalytics.ts, and a live VSAC value-set service (vsacService.ts). But the explicit gaps are precisely the items that gate category leadership and payer/ACO sales: no CQL execution engine (measures are bespoke SQL, not shareable or verifiable against canonical eCQM logic), no QI-Core/US Core profiling, no FHIR Measure/MeasureReport or $evaluate-measure operation, no QRDA or DEQM submission path, no Bulk FHIR ($export) ingestion, no SMART App Launch (v2.2.0) for in-EHR embedding, no SMART Backend Services, and an HCC seed that must be aligned to V28 (not legacy V24). The actionable thesis: adopt the FHIR data contract (US Core/QI-Core) and a CQL execution engine as the foundation, then layer DEQM/Gaps-in-Care, CRD/DTR/PAS, and Da Vinci Risk Adjustment + Member Attribution on top of capabilities Medgnosis largely already has — turning compliance mandates into a differentiated, standards-native product that sells into providers, ACOs, and payers simultaneously, with Inferno/Touchstone conformance as the proof of correctness.

ASTP/ONC estimates ~4,000 hours saved per certified developer in year one (CORRECTION: the draft's '~1.4M industry compliance hours' figure could not be verified; replaced with this confirmed estimate). - **CMS-0057-F mandates four production FHIR APIs by Jan 1, 2027 plus prior-auth process/metrics changes starting Jan 1, 2026** — Published Jan 17, 2024. Impacted payers = Medicare Advantage organizations, state Medicaid/CHIP FFS, Medicaid/CHIP managed care, and QHP issuers on FFEs (not standalone dental, not employer/commercial). Required by Jan 1, 2027: (1) expanded Patient Access API (adds prior-auth data, excluding drugs), (2) new Provider Access API, (3) Payer-to-Payer API (replaces the never-enforced CMS-9115 version), (4) Prior Authorization API. Starting Jan 1, 2026: PA decision timeframes (72h expedited / 7 calendar days standard for non-MA items/services), specific-denial-reason requirement, and public PA metrics reporting (first metrics reporting in 2026, by Mar 31). CMS exercises enforcement discretion to NOT require X12 278 when the FHIR Prior Authorization API is used. Implementing IGs are recommended but not strictly mandated, giving payers latitude on the exact Da Vinci versions.
  - _Evidence:_ cms.gov/cms-interoperability-and-prior-authorization-final-rule-cms-0057-f; CMS 3-26-24 presentation PDF; Myers & Stauffer and Firely (fire.ly) timeline analyses
- **The prior-auth IG trio (CRD + DTR + PAS) is the technical realization of CMS's Prior Authorization API and is directly buildable from Medgnosis's existing CDS Hooks + rules engine** — CRD uses CDS Hooks (order-select/order-sign hooks) to surface coverage requirements at order time; DTR delivers SMART-app FHIR Questionnaires with embedded CQL to auto-populate documentation; PAS (v2.1.0 published / STU2; v2.2.0-ballot generated 2025-07-29) sends FHIR prior-auth requests and bridges to X12 278 where required. Workflow: CRD identifies the requirement → DTR gathers documentation → PAS submits. CRD is structurally identical to Medgnosis's existing CDS Hooks 2.0 discovery + order-sign service (apps/api/src/routes/cds-hooks/index.ts); this is a wrap, not a rebuild. DTR's CQL dependency is the same CQL engine gap that blocks Medgnosis's quality-measure story — solving CQL once unlocks both.
  - _Evidence:_ hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-pas (v2.1.0); hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-crd; hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-dtr; CMS X12 278 enforcement discretion in CMS-0057-F
- **Digital quality measurement is consolidating on QI-Core + CQL + cqf-measures IG v5.0.0 + DEQM v5.0.0, with DEQM reporting positioned to replace QRDA I/III** — The Quality Measure (cqf-measures) IG v5.0.0 defines eCQM structure using FHIR Measure + Library resources (replacing HQMF); QI-Core (current v6.0.0, built on US Core) is the data model for dQM authoring and CDS; CQL is the shared logic language; the $evaluate-measure operation generates a MeasureReport. DEQM v5.0.0 (STU5, package generated 2025-05-19) individual + summary reporting is built from US Core/QI-Core and explicitly 'replaces QRDA Category I and III reporting'; DEQM Gaps in Care reporting enables payer-to-provider open-gap exchange. CMS opened public comment on draft CMS FHIR dQMs — 49 Eligible Clinician + 17 Hospital Inpatient + 4 Hospital Outpatient = 70 measures — closing Feb 25, 2026 (5pm ET) via the ONC Jira tracker, and intends FHIR-receiving quality data systems by ~2027. CORRECTION: Medgnosis already has measureEvaluator.ts/measureCalculatorV2.ts, but they execute bespoke SQL, not canonical CQL against FHIR Measure resources — so the gap is CQL+FHIR-Measure conformance, not measurement capability per se.
  - _Evidence:_ hl7.org/fhir/us/cqfmeasures v5.0.0; hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-deqm v5.0.0; hl7.org/fhir/us/qicore v6.0.0; ecqi.healthit.gov draft CMS FHIR dQM public comment notice (closes 2026-02-25)
- **CMS 'Universal Foundation' of aligned measures is the policy engine pushing every CMS program toward a common, digital, FHIR-based measure core** — Introduced in NEJM (Fischer/Seshamani/Jacobs, 2023, 10.1056/NEJMp2215539), the Universal Foundation is a set of aligned quality measures applied across CMS quality/value programs (MSSP, MIPS/MVPs, MA Star Ratings, Medicaid Adult/Child Core Sets) to reduce measure proliferation. CMS is implementing relevant Universal Foundation measures across programs and building FHIR-capable quality data systems, coordinated with the Core Quality Measures Collaborative (CQMC, an AHIP+CMS-convened partnership now stewarded by NCQA) for public/private payer alignment. A platform that natively executes the canonical digital versions of these specific measures gains durable leverage; Medgnosis should map its 354+ measures to the Universal Foundation set and prioritize CQL conformance for those.
  - _Evidence:_ NEJM 10.1056/NEJMp2215539; CMS National Quality Strategy; CMS Quality Conference 2026 (ecqi.healthit.gov); Core Quality Measures Collaborative (NCQA/CQMC)
- **TEFCA is live with ELEVEN designated QHINs (mid-2026) and a FHIR (SMART/Bulk) roadmap; Carequality/CommonWell are converging toward TEFCA** — CORRECTION: The Sequoia Project (TEFCA RCE) lists ELEVEN designated QHINs as of mid-2026, not ~10. - **CMS-HCC risk adjustment has fully transitioned to model V28 (100% V28 for PY2026), changing the financial logic of every VBC/ACO/MA use case Medgnosis touches** — Phase-in: PY2024 = 67% V24 / 33% V28; PY2025 = 33% V24 / 67% V28; PY2026 = 100% V28. V28 uses an updated ICD-10→HCC mapping, a revised/renumbered HCC list, and constrains/excludes coefficients for HCCs CMS deemed subject to coding variation. CY2026 also uses 100% updated RxHCC (recalibrated) and the updated ESRD models (dialysis, functioning graft, transplant). Medgnosis's hccAnalytics.ts coding-review seed must target V28 mappings, not legacy V24, to be commercially relevant; the Da Vinci Risk Adjustment IG provides the FHIR exchange substrate for closing suspected-HCC gaps with payers.

Domains include food insecurity, housing instability, transportation, financial strain, social isolation, interpersonal violence, education, employment, and veteran status. SDOH is a USCDI data class (a certification baseline element since v3). ICD-10-CM Z-codes (Z55-Z65) are the billing-side capture mechanism. Gravity also feeds CMS health-equity measures and the screening requirements emerging in IQR/MIPS.
  - _Evidence:_ hl7.org/fhir/us/sdoh-clinicalcare; healthit.gov/isa SDOH data class; thegravityproject.net; ICD-10-CM Z55-Z65

Bulk export typically returns the US Core data set. SMART App Launch (EHR + standalone launch, scopes, OAuth 2.0/OpenID Connect) is required by §170.315(g)(10) and is how analytics/CDS apps embed in Epic/Cerner. ONC's Inferno (plus HL7 Touchstone) is the conformance test harness CMS/ONC point to for g(10) and IG conformance. This is the path for Medgnosis to ingest 1M+ patients from EHRs/QHINs at scale and to be launchable inside an EHR.

- **Information blocking is now actively enforced with real financial teeth against both developers and providers, with a Sept/Oct 2025 OIG+ASTP enforcement alert** — The Cures Act info-blocking rule defines 8 exceptions (Preventing Harm, Privacy, Security, Infeasibility, Health IT Performance, Content & Manner, Fees, Licensing). NEW (not in draft): in late Sept/early Oct 2025, HHS-OIG and ASTP/ONC jointly issued an Information Blocking Enforcement Alert signaling active enforcement, priority conduct, and reporting pathways. Implication: a platform that hoards or fails to expose EHI via standard APIs creates customer regulatory exposure — interoperability is now a liability-mitigation feature, not just a convenience.

ASTP releases a draft each January and finalizes each July. Successive versions add equity/SO-GI, SDOH, functional/disability/mental-cognitive status, Care Plan, Portable Medical Order, and Unique Device Identifier elements. Medgnosis should treat USCDI v6 as the floor and track v7.
  - _Evidence:_ isp.healthit.gov USCDI; ASTP/ONC Standards Bulletin 2025-2; Draft USCDI v7 overview (healthit.gov, Jan/Feb 2026); USCDI v6 published 2025-07-24
- **NEW — Da Vinci PDex, Member Attribution List (ATR), and Risk Adjustment IGs complete the payer-facing FHIR stack the draft underweighted** — Beyond CRD/DTR/PAS and DEQM, the payer-data side of CMS-0057-F leans on: Payer Data Exchange (PDex) — the IG behind Patient Access, Provider Access, and Payer-to-Payer clinical data exchange (uses US Core + PDex profiles, $member-match); PDex US Drug Formulary and PDex Plan-Net (provider directory) for Patient Access; Member Attribution (ATR) List for sharing attributed-member rosters between payers and value-based-care providers; and the Da Vinci Risk Adjustment IG for coding-gap (suspected-HCC) closure over FHIR. - SVAP (Standards Version Advancement Process) — voluntary advancement to newer IG/USCDI versions ahead of rulemaking
- Conformance: ONC Inferno (g(10)/IG test kit) + HL7 Touchstone (Connectathon test platform)

### Frontier & Trends

- Deregulatory pivot: ASTP/ONC is thinning functional certification (HTI-5 removes 34/updates 7 of ~60 criteria, drops §170.315(d)) while retaining §170.315(g)(10) — the federal floor is collapsing onto FHIR APIs + USCDI content + SMART/OAuth + Bulk FHIR. Standards mastery, not certification breadth, is the moat.
- Prior-auth automation is the near-term commercial battleground: CMS-0057-F PA process changes hit Jan 1, 2026 and the FHIR PA API hits Jan 1, 2027, making CRD/DTR/PAS the highest-ROI Da Vinci build for any platform with a CDS Hooks + rules substrate.
- Quality measurement is migrating from HQMF/QRDA to FHIR Measure + CQL + DEQM; CMS's 70 draft FHIR dQMs (49 EC / 17 HIP / 4 HOP, comments close Feb 25, 2026) signal the production transition and a 2027 FHIR-receiving target.
- TEFCA is maturing fast — 11 QHINs, additions of Oracle/Surescripts/Netsmart, and a FHIR/Bulk roadmap — turning national exchange into a viable bulk longitudinal-data acquisition channel rather than only point-to-point document pulls.
- AI/Predictive-DSI transparency is becoming a baseline expectation (HTI-1 31-attribute Predictive DSI disclosure + IRM); explainable, source-attributed CDS is shifting from differentiator to table stakes, favoring Medgnosis's logic-as-data/explain architecture.
- Risk adjustment fully on V28 for PY2026 plus the Da Vinci Risk Adjustment IG is pushing suspected-HCC gap closure into standardized FHIR payer↔provider exchange.
- Information-blocking enforcement is activating: the Sept/Oct 2025 OIG+ASTP joint Enforcement Alert plus live provider disincentives and $1M CMPs mean openness/API-exposure is now a liability-mitigation requirement.
- Convergence of CDS, quality, and prior-auth on ONE data contract (US Core/QI-Core) and ONE logic language (CQL) — the platforms that unify these win cross-program leverage; fragmented bespoke logic loses.

### Risks & Pitfalls

- Bespoke-SQL measures are not portable or verifiable against canonical eCQM/CQL logic — payers and ACOs will not trust quality numbers that can't be reconciled to CMS's FHIR Measure + CQL specs; this blocks payer/ACO sales regardless of feature richness.
- Building CRD/DTR/PAS or PDex against the wrong IG version (e.g., PAS 2.0.0 vs 2.1.0, or pre-v6 QI-Core) risks payer-side rejection; pin to published versions and track ballots (PAS 2.2.0-ballot, Bulk Data v3 ballot) without prematurely adopting them.
- CMS-0057-F does not strictly mandate specific Da Vinci IG versions, so payers vary in what they accept — a single rigid implementation may fail real-world payer onboarding; design for version negotiation/CapabilityStatement-driven behavior.
- HCC seed drift: shipping V24-based coding suggestions in a 100%-V28 (PY2026) world produces financially wrong gap recommendations and erodes credibility with risk-adjustment buyers.
- Outdated USCDI assumption: the draft's reliance on v4/v5 is stale — v4 adoption was WITHDRAWN and v6 (July 2025) is the current floor; building to a withdrawn version wastes effort.
- TEFCA roster/feature assumptions go stale quickly (11 QHINs now, FHIR/Bulk roadmap evolving) — verify the live Sequoia list and current QTF/Common Agreement before architecting a QHIN integration.
- Information-blocking exposure flows to customers: if Medgnosis becomes a chokepoint that fails to expose EHI via standard APIs, provider/HIN customers inherit CMP/disincentive risk — openness must be designed in, not bolted on.
- Predictive-DSI transparency is not optional theater: incomplete or stale source-attribute/IRM disclosure for any AI/risk model could become an information-blocking or certification credibility issue for embedding partners.
- CQL execution is a real engineering lift (a CQL engine + FHIR data layer + value-set resolution via VSAC); underestimating it (treating it as 'just another rules format') is the single biggest schedule risk.
- Conformance debt: claiming standards support without passing Inferno/Touchstone leads to failed payer/EHR integrations late in sales cycles — bake conformance testing into CI.

### Implications for Medgnosis

- Stand up a CQL execution engine as the foundational investment: integrate an open-source CQL engine (e.g., cqframework cql-execution / clinical-quality-language, or DBCG cql-engine via a JVM/Java sidecar) backed by a FHIR data layer; resolve value sets through the existing vsacService.ts. This single capability unblocks DTR, FHIR dQMs, and verifiable eCQMs at once.
- Profile Medgnosis's internal model to US Core + QI-Core v6.0.0 and expose a FHIR R4 facade (extend routes/fhir/index.ts) — the one data contract that serves measures, CDS Hooks/CRD, Bulk ingest, and prior auth. Prioritize the resources QI-Core/US Core require for the Universal Foundation measures.
- Emit FHIR Measure + Library + MeasureReport and implement the $evaluate-measure operation: refactor measureEvaluator.ts/measureCalculatorV2.ts to produce canonical MeasureReports (individual + summary) so output is reconcilable with CMS specs; map the 354+ measures to the 70 draft CMS FHIR dQMs and Universal Foundation set.
- Build DEQM v5.0.0 Gaps-in-Care producer/responder on the existing 45-bundle, 354+-measure care-gap engine (routes/care-gaps) — the lowest-effort, highest-value payer-facing capability; add DEQM individual/summary reporting to replace QRDA I/III submission paths.
- Wrap the existing CDS Hooks 2.0 service (routes/cds-hooks) as Da Vinci CRD (order-select/order-sign coverage discovery), then add DTR (SMART app + FHIR Questionnaire + CQL prepopulation) and PAS v2.1.0 (FHIR PA request with X12 278 bridge). This is the CMS-0057-F prior-auth product, mostly a wrap of existing substrate plus the new CQL engine.
- Implement SMART Backend Services + Bulk Data $export (NDJSON) ingestion and SMART App Launch v2.2.0 (EHR + standalone) so Medgnosis can both ingest 1M+ patients from EHRs/QHINs at scale and launch embedded inside Epic/Cerner — required to be procurement-viable.
- Reseed hccAnalytics.ts to CMS-HCC V28 mappings (drop V24) for PY2026 relevance, and add a Da Vinci Risk Adjustment IG path for FHIR-based suspected-HCC coding-gap closure with payers; add Da Vinci Member Attribution (ATR) roster import/export to anchor a VBC product line.
- Make Predictive-DSI transparency a first-class feature: surface HTI-1-style source attributes (13 evidence-based / 31 predictive) and IRM-style risk summaries for every Medgnosis risk model and rule via the explain/transparency endpoints — turn the logic-as-data architecture into a marketed AI-transparency differentiator.
- Adopt Gravity SDOH Clinical Care coding (LOINC/SNOMED/ICD-10-CM Z55-Z65) end-to-end so SDOH drives equity-stratified care gaps and risk scores and satisfies the USCDI SDOH data class.
- Bake conformance into CI: run ONC Inferno g(10)/US Core/SMART/Bulk suites and validate Da Vinci IG conformance (Touchstone/Connectathon) as automated gates — make standards correctness provable for payer/EHR procurement.
- Pursue a QHIN-connected ingestion path (via a designated QHIN or QHIN-connected HIE) under the Treatment/Operations/Individual-Access XPs to acquire longitudinal records at national scale, tracking the live Sequoia QHIN list and current QTF/Common Agreement v2.x.
- Treat openness as liability mitigation: ensure Medgnosis never becomes an EHI chokepoint — expose standard APIs and document Content-and-Manner/Fees/Licensing posture so customers are shielded from info-blocking disincentives/CMPs in light of the Sept/Oct 2025 OIG+ASTP enforcement alert.
